_## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY_

_This report details a network of suspicious and unusual transactions related to a criminal enterprise engaged in money laundering, tax evasion, and fraud. The activities involve the abuse of at least eight ABSA bank accounts, complex inter-company transfers, and the use of identity fraud to control financial systems, all constituting reportable offenses under the Financial Intelligence Centre Act (FICA)._

_The primary suspects, **Peter Andrew Faucitt, Rynette Farrar, and Daniel Jacobus Bantjies**, are knowing participants whose foreknowledge is proven by documentary evidence. They have established and utilized a web of entities to conceal the proceeds of crime._

_The failure of these individuals, in their capacities as directors and accountants (accountable institutions), to file Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) in the face of overwhelming evidence of financial crime is, in itself, a criminal offense under FICA. This report is submitted to rectify that failure and to request an immediate investigation by the FIC._

_## 3. GROUNDS FOR SUSPICION (SECTION 29 OF FICA)_

_### 3.1 Suspicion of Money Laundering (Proceeds of Unlawful Activities)_

_**Unlawful Activities:** Fraud, Theft, Tax Evasion, Racketeering (POCA)._

_**Suspicious Transactions:**_
_1.  **Asset Stripping:** Transfer of over R2.5M from RegimA SA’s bank account between Oct 2024 and Mar 2025, leaving it with a balance of R5,284 (a 99.8% reduction)._
_2.  **Use of Multiple Accounts:** Utilization of at least **eight ABSA bank accounts** to layer and integrate illicit funds._
_3.  **Complex Inter-Company Transfers:** Funds were moved between various entities without legitimate business justification._

_### 3.2 Suspicion of Tax Evasion_

_**Suspicious Transactions:**_
_1.  **Concealment of Revenue:** The diversion of an R8.5M annual revenue stream away from the declared entity (RegimA SA) to other accounts to evade corporate income tax and VAT._

_### 3.3 Transactions with No Apparent Business or Lawful Purpose_

_The rapid and near-total depletion of a profitable company’s bank account and the circular movement of funds between entities are inconsistent with normal business operations._
