# Conduct-Contrast Schedule: Leave-to-Appeal Judgment

**Date:** 11 May 2026  
**Source judgment:** 4 May 2026 leave-to-appeal judgment by Labuschagne J  
**Primary links:** [EVENT 179](../../docs/events/EVENT_179.md), [Cycle 10 Alexander Report](../../docs/reports/KSM_LEX_ALEXANDER_CYCLE10_2026-05-11.md), [Outcome Note](../../docs/filings/LEAVE_TO_APPEAL_JUDGMENT_OUTCOME_NOTE_2026-05-11.md)

## Purpose

This schedule operationalizes the Cycle 10 **Judicial Closure and Conduct Contrast** transformation. It translates the leave-to-appeal judgment into a controlled filing layer that distinguishes what the applicant alleged, what the judgment actually held, and where each finding may safely be used without overstating the criminal burden.[1] [2]

| Applicant position or pressure point | Judgment-linked contrast | Filing use | Do-not-overstate boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clause 2.5 allegedly excluded Mrs Faucitt from involvement in the businesses. | The judgment records that clause 2.5 prohibits prejudicial conduct and does not preclude involvement in the businesses in any manner. | High Court and CIPC governance-correction point. | Not a universal merits finding on every business dispute. |
| Stock and training incidents allegedly constituted contempt. | The judgment records that the respondent version was accepted on motion-proceedings principles and that breach was not established by mere say-so. | Civil-procedural proof of failed contempt foundation. | Not equivalent to affirmative disproving of every underlying fact for every forum. |
| November 2025 affidavits were relied on to sustain later urgent relief. | The judgment records that their relevance to the later urgent application was not demonstrated. | Rebuttal against recycled-affidavit pressure. | Pair with source affidavits before alleging deliberate falsehood. |
| Leave to appeal was allegedly justified. | The judgment records no reasonable prospects and no other compelling reason for appeal. | Close the immediate appeal-risk node. | Treat any later petition as a new event. |
| Costs consequences were wholly respondent-vindicating. | The judgment awards Scale C costs against the applicant but separately allocates wasted 16 April costs to Mrs Faucitt. | Use a nuanced costs chronology. | Do not omit the wasted-costs qualification. |
