# KSM-LEX-DGen-Alexander Cycle: Rule 6(12)(c) Reconsideration Application

This report applies the **KSM-LEX-DGen-Alexander** cycle to the transcribed **Notice of Motion and Affidavit in Support of Rule 6(12)(c) Reconsideration Application**. The purpose is not to rewrite the filed document, but to strengthen the case architecture around the document by identifying the strongest legal centers, sharpening the entities and relations, refining the event chronology, and recommending how the GitHub Pages evidence record should present the application as one of the three active litigation streams and as part of the whole evidence corpus.[1]

> **Central KSM finding.** The reconsideration application is structurally strongest when framed as a proportionality and governance-restoration application: an ex parte preservatory order granted on alleged financial/governance concerns has, on the applicant’s version, evolved into an indefinite exclusionary governance mechanism, financial-disclosure barrier, litigation-pressure instrument, and patrimonial-prejudice engine. That framing should remain distinct from, but cross-referenced to, broader civil, criminal, CIPC, POPIA, commercial-crime, and tax-fraud pathways.

The analysis below treats the transcribed document as the primary record. It preserves the transcription QA finding that both PDF copies contain the same late-document internal page-order anomaly: internal affidavit page 41 appears before internal page 40, followed by internal page 42. The source order should remain preserved in the evidence package, with the anomaly documented wherever the affidavit is cited.[1]

## 2. Observe Wholeness

The current evidentiary field has four nested centers. At the smallest scale are specific transactions, communications, and procedural events: the alleged R500,000 transaction, FNB mandate correspondence, bank-card cancellation, September 2025 transfers, client redirection circular, contempt applications, and stock-room incidents.[1] At the middle scale are recurring patterns: exclusion from governance, denial of financial information, financial starvation, use of contempt proceedings, and operational conflict around stock and property access.[1] At the macro scale is the applicant’s core proposition that the interim order is no longer preservatory but has become exclusionary, disproportionate, and functionally equivalent to removal without final adjudication under Companies Act mechanisms.[1]

The wholeness is strong because the Notice of Motion asks for remedies that map closely to the factual centers in the affidavit: reconsideration or variation, restoration of governance participation, access to financial information, independent financial oversight, stock-facility regulation, and costs.[1]

## 3. Identify Centers

The strongest center is the **changed-circumstances and proportionality center**. The application alleges that the forensic audit has been completed, no meaningful delinquency proceedings have materialised, and the audit report does not establish fraud, theft, dishonesty, or misconduct sufficient to justify continued exclusion.[1] This is the center most directly aligned with Rule 6(12)(c), because it asks whether the continued operation of an order granted in absence remains justified.

The second strong center is the **financial transparency and patrimonial-protection center**. The applicant alleges that she remains a director/member/shareholder and patrimonial stakeholder, yet has been denied bank statements, management accounts, VAT submissions, financial statements, payroll summaries, and operational information.[1] This center is particularly important because it links corporate governance to the divorce action and the joint estate.

The third strong center is the **financial-abuse and litigation-asymmetry center**. The affidavit alleges withheld remuneration, refusal of loan-account drawdowns, company-funded legal payments for the First Respondent, repeated contempt applications, and diversion of the applicant’s UK pension into the First Respondent’s UK bank account.[1] This center supports urgency, balance of convenience, and proportionality.

## 4. Detect Latent Center

The latent center wanting to emerge is **judicial stewardship of corporate and patrimonial information pending final adjudication**. The present application should not be forced to carry every allegation from the broader revenue-stream-hijacking matter. Its living center is narrower and more judicially digestible: the Court can preserve both sides’ rights by ordering regulated participation, financial transparency, independent oversight, and stock-arrangement controls without deciding the full merits of fraud, delinquency, divorce, or criminal culpability.

This latent center creates a bridge between the reconsideration application and future complaints. It allows the Rule 6(12)(c) application to function as an evidence gateway: if bank statements, VAT submissions, payroll summaries, circulars, and mandate records are disclosed, those materials can then be used in civil pleadings, CIPC complaints, POPIA complaints, commercial-crime submissions, or NPA/SARS reports according to their burden-of-proof profile.

## 7. Inspect Structure

The existing filing shape should be preserved. The Notice of Motion already reflects the correct architecture: urgency, reconsideration/setting aside or variation, access to financial information, independent audit/oversight, costs, and further relief.[1] The affidavit then supplies the factual spine: jurisdiction, summary of relief, original order purpose, audit report, reconsideration law, financial transparency, remuneration, divorce prejudice, trust allegations, threats/interference, requirements for interim relief, urgency, and conclusion.[1]

The structural risk is not absence of evidence. The risk is **over-compression of multiple forums into one affidavit**. The reconsideration application should remain a procedural and proportionality vehicle. Broader allegations of revenue hijacking, POPIA violations, tax prejudice, or criminal conduct should be referenced only insofar as they explain why disclosure and oversight are necessary.

## 10. Observe Mirror

The mirror test asks whether the transformation serves truth, proportionality, and judicial accessibility. The proposed refinement does so because it avoids premature escalation. It does not require a court in the Rule 6(12)(c) application to determine every criminal or regulatory issue. Instead, it asks the court to resolve the immediate procedural imbalance and to order disclosure/oversight measures capable of either proving or disproving the broader allegations.

This is ethically important. The broader matter contains serious allegations, but the present document’s strongest court-ready proposition is narrower: **a temporary ex parte order should not be allowed to become indefinite de facto corporate removal, financial exclusion, or litigation pressure without final adjudication and without transparent governance information**.[1]

## 14. Filing Refinement Recommendations

The most appropriate filing refinement is not a new broad criminal complaint based only on this document. The immediate refinement should be a **supporting schedule package** for the reconsideration application. That package should include: a one-page chronology, a relief-evidence matrix, a bank-account information request/refusal schedule, a company-role schedule, and a limited operational stock-room safety protocol. These items would make the application easier for the court to understand and harder to mischaracterise as an attempt to relitigate every broader dispute.

For CIPC and Companies Act complaints, the document should be used to support a focused complaint that the practical effect of the interim order and subsequent conduct may have bypassed statutory governance mechanisms. For POPIA and commercial-crime pathways, this document should be treated as a source of leads and sworn allegations, but not as the sole proof source. For NPA/SARS tax-fraud reporting, the document identifies potential accounting and tax concerns, but ledger, VAT, SARS, bank-statement, and audit-report exhibits must be assembled before any 95% criminal-proof style assertion is made.
