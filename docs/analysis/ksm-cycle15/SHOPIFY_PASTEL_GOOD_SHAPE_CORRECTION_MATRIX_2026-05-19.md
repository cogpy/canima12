# Cycle 15 Good Shape Correction Matrix: Shopify Order Rerouting to Pastel

**Date:** 2026-05-19  
**KSM-LEX-DGen-Alexander property:** Good Shape (Alexander Property 6)  
**Purpose:** This matrix converts the live evidence model from loose shorthand such as “Shopify deletion” or “Shopify destruction” into the more precise factual formulation: **Shopify orders were secretly rerouted to Pastel, producing audit-trail severance from the perspective of the legitimate directors and disabling the practical evidentiary continuity of the Shopify platform**.[1] [2]

> **Canonical correction.** The evidentiary claim should not be framed as a bare assertion that Shopify records were literally deleted unless a specific deletion log proves that narrower allegation. The current Good Shape formulation is that the **orders were rerouted from Shopify to Pastel**, producing revenue diversion, platform-control impairment, and practical audit-trail severance from the legitimate directors’ perspective.[1] [2]

## 3. Interpretation Rule for Older Drafts

Older drafts remain historically useful because they preserve the development path of the case theory. However, where older files use wording such as **“Shopify destruction,” “deleted orders,” “records destroyed,”** or **“audit trail destroyed,”** they should now be read through the following interpretation rule:

> In live filings and current reference pages, “destruction” means **practical audit-trail severance or concealment** unless a specific source document proves literal deletion. The actionable current event is **secret order rerouting to Pastel**, with forensic recovery required from Shopify, Pastel/Sage, relevant email instructions, access logs, and accounting exports.

This rule preserves the evidentiary substance but strengthens adversarial robustness. It prevents the opponent from defeating a broader revenue-diversion claim merely by showing that some raw Shopify records still exist.
