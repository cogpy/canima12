The case record is now strong enough that the main problem is no longer raw evidence accumulation. The main problem is **cognitive grip**: whether the evidence, chronology, entity mapping, filing thresholds, and rebuttal language remain sufficiently integrated under adversarial pressure. The existing April 2026 refinement work already identifies the dominant risks as presentation risk, chronology compression, entity slippage, attachment dependency, and process-overload reframing.[1] [2] [3] [4]

Applying the **optimal-cognitive-grip** lens to the current **LEX-DigiTwin-NN** workflow sharpens that diagnosis. On that reading, the live repository is already materially advanced, but four bottlenecks still govern the next quality jump: the need for stronger **testimonial / institutional corroboration** at criminal edges, stricter **annexure-dependency control**, continued **entity normalization**, and more explicit **process atomization** for August 2025 and March 2026.[1] [2] [4] [5]

## The Four Dominant Bottlenecks

### 1. Testimonial and institutional corroboration bottleneck

The repository already recognizes that documentary strength is high, but that certain criminal edges remain more robust when supported by institutional or witness confirmation rather than documentary inference alone.[4] The optimal-cognitive-grip reading therefore treats **FNB**, **Sage**, **Stock2Shop**, and key operational witnesses not as generic improvements, but as the principal route for converting near-threshold arguments into defensible beyond-reasonable-doubt structures.[1] [4]

### 2. Annexure-dependency bottleneck

The newly integrated **16 March 2026 supplementary founding affidavit** improves the chronology but also demonstrates a classic annexure-dependency problem. The affidavit proves that the applicant relied on **PF36** and **PF37**, but it does not independently prove every proposition said to arise from them.[3] [5] Under the composite lens, that means the filing surface must distinguish between **directly proved pleading posture** and **separately proved underlying fact**.

### 3. Entity-normalization bottleneck

The April refinement cycle and the supplementary-affidavit integration both point to the same conclusion: the case becomes vulnerable whenever it relies on loose ordinal or shorthand identifiers rather than company-specific legal identity.[2] [3] This matters most where bank control, extraction, VAT, tax exposure, and post-order prejudice are alleged.

### 4. Process-atomization bottleneck

The strongest rebuttal language in the live surface already depends on keeping procedural dates distinct: forgery versus backdating, affidavit versus filing, order versus enforcement, judgment versus appeal.[2] [4] The optimal-cognitive-grip layer confirms that this is not a stylistic preference. It is the core mechanism by which the case resists defence attempts to collapse multiple events into one allegedly muddled grievance narrative.
