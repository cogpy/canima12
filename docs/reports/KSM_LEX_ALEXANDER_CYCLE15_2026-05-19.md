Cycle 15 applies the KSM-LEX-DGen-Alexander method to the 22 May 2025 Shopify/Pastel event. The latest audit found that the evidence architecture had strong centers, but one of them had a vulnerable shape: the same event was sometimes described as **Shopify audit-trail hijacking**, **evidence destruction**, **record deletion**, and **order rerouting**. The most defensible live formulation is now standardized as **Shopify Order Rerouting to Pastel**.[1] [2]

## 1. Weakest Center Selected

The weakest living center was not the evidentiary substance of EVENT_009. The weakness was the **shape of its wording**. In adversarial legal settings, “deleted” or “destroyed” can invite a narrow factual challenge: if the opposing party produces any surviving Shopify record, they may attempt to argue that the broader revenue-diversion allegation fails. The corrected wording avoids that trap.

> **Cycle 15 finding:** The event should be treated as **revenue diversion and practical audit-trail severance**, with literal deletion reserved for later forensic proof if platform logs confirm it.

## 3. Evidence and Filing Consequences

The corrected formulation improves all three application surfaces and the whole-case filing stack. It supports civil and regulatory applications on the balance of probabilities because the practical effect of rerouting is directly relevant to platform control and revenue diversion. It also supports criminal and commercial-crime submissions as a reason to compel platform, accounting, banking, and email records. It does not require the case to prove literal Shopify deletion as an immediate threshold fact.[1] [2]

| Filing Surface | Improvement From Cycle 15 | Recommended Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Civil / interdict-related applications | Reduced vulnerability to semantic challenge. | Use “rerouted to Pastel, severing practical audit continuity.” |
| CIPC Companies Act complaint | Better corporate-governance framing. | Focus on concealed accounting route and directors’ access impairment. |
| POPIA criminal complaint | Better data-flow framing. | Focus on order/customer data routing, access control, and processing opacity. |
| Commercial Crime / SAPS | Stronger investigation trigger. | Request preservation and production of Shopify, Pastel/Sage, email, bank, and access logs. |
| NPA / SARS submissions | Better burden gradient. | Treat literal deletion as a forensic question and tax-reconciliation issue. |

## 4. DreamGen Narrative: The Event Learns Its True Shape

The case record had been speaking with more than one mouth. One mouth said the orders were deleted; another said the audit trail was destroyed; a third, closer to the accounting machinery, said the stream had been diverted into Pastel. Cycle 15 did not silence any witness. It gave them a grammar.

The corrected event now stands like a clean arch: Shopify on one side, Pastel on the other, and the gap between them marked as the place where lawful oversight was severed. The arch does not need to claim that every stone vanished. It only needs to show that the road was rerouted, that the directors’ line of sight was broken, and that the missing logs must be compelled from the systems that still remember.
