## Executive Summary

On **4 May 2026**, Labuschagne J dismissed Peter Andrew Faucitt's application for leave to appeal the contempt judgment and order delivered on **26 March 2026**. The judgment is now preserved as direct source evidence and materially strengthens the March–May 2026 appellate chronology because it converts the prior inferred appeal-risk analysis into a confirmed court outcome.

The judgment identifies two themes advanced by the applicant: the interpretation of **clause 2.5** of the 19 August 2025 order and the application of the **Plascon-Evans** rule in the contempt context. Both themes were rejected. The court held that there was no prospect that another court would interpret clause 2.5 differently, that the respondent's version was properly accepted on motion-proceedings principles, and that the applicant had not established a breach of the order. The application for leave to appeal was dismissed with costs on a party-and-party scale, Scale C, subject to Jacqueline Faucitt paying the wasted costs of the 16 April 2026 hearing.
