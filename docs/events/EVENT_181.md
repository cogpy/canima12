## Summary

The payment extract records **sixteen payments** from **RegimA Skin Treatments CC**, ABSA account **4112241409**, to **Elliott Attorneys** between **25 August 2025** and **15 April 2026**, totalling **R478,957.93**. This expands the earlier [EVT-073](./EVT-073.md) legal-cost event from a single-payment snapshot into a sustained legal-funding pattern.

The event is significant because Elliott Attorneys appears elsewhere in the evidence repository as the applicant-side law firm in proceedings and correspondence adverse to Jax, including contempt-related pressure. The existing [Abuse of Process Analysis](./ABUSE_OF_PROCESS_ANALYSIS.md) already frames the broader mechanism as a **circular trap**: Jax’s access to company funds was restricted while company funds were allegedly used to finance litigation pressure against her.

## Why this matters

If RST had a valid, unconflicted corporate mandate for each invoice, the payments may be defensible as ordinary legal costs. If, however, RST funds were used to finance a personal or factional litigation campaign against Jax while she was forced to pay personally, the payments support the following legal characterisations:

| Characterisation | Evidentiary use |
|---|---|
| Improper-purpose corporate expenditure | Supports civil and CIPC/CC fiduciary-duty complaints. |
| Inequality of arms / abuse of process | Supports rescission, postponement, punitive-cost, and de bonis propriis arguments. |
| Conflict-of-interest and mandate ambiguity | Supports Legal Practice Council complaint and disclosure requests. |
| Possible misappropriation or fraud | Only if mandate, authority, deception, and intent are independently proved. |

## Required follow-up evidence

The record should be strengthened by obtaining Elliott Attorneys’ engagement letter, invoices, trust ledger entries, fee notes, written instructions, payment approvals, RST member resolutions, conflict-check records, and correspondence identifying the client for each invoice. These materials are essential before any criminal-proof assertion is made.
