## Description

Hon. Justice Labuschagne handed down judgment and order in Case 2025/137857 on **26 March 2026** in contempt proceedings linked to the **19 August 2025** interim interdict granted by Kumalo J. The judgment records that the applicant sought, on supplemented papers, to enrol a **09 February 2026** contempt application urgently in the week of **16 to 20 March 2026**.

The court identified **clause 2.5** of the August 2025 order as the operative paragraph for the contempt case. That clause interdicted the first and second respondents from dealing with the business and administration of the third to sixth respondents, their employees, partners, and clientele in any manner that may cause prejudice.

The judgment then dealt directly with the factual incidents relied upon by the applicant: stocktake / stock-release disputes involving **Mr Mphande**, training-session disputes involving **Cherie Smith**, **Ms Farrar**, and **Ms Gayane Williams**, and alleged operational interference by the first respondent. On the court's reading of the papers, those incidents did **not** establish the necessary breach and wilfulness for contempt.

## Direct Judicial Findings

1. The applicant bore the burden of establishing contempt in proceedings seeking criminal sanctions.
2. Because the matter proceeded on motion papers, the court expressly applied the logic of factual dispute resolution and held that contempt had to be established on the respondent's version unless that version fell to be rejected.
3. The court found that the evidence did **not** establish disruption of stocktake or delivery by the first respondent.
4. The training incidents were treated as disputed operational events rather than proven wilful defiance.
5. Affidavits by **Mr Mphande** and **Mr Tshabalala** were expressly relied upon as support for the first respondent's version.
6. The court held that the applicant had established the existence of the order and the respondent's knowledge of it, but had **not** established either **breach** or **wilfulness**.

## Order and Outcome

The court dismissed the application and directed the applicant to pay costs on the scale as between attorney and client.

## Significance

1. This is a directly evidenced **first-instance judicial rejection** of the contempt theory.
2. It narrows the operative dispute to **clarity of prohibition**, **proof of breach**, and **wilfulness**, rather than abstract claims of practical control.
3. It strengthens respondent-side arguments that the applicant was attempting to convert a contested business-management conflict into quasi-criminal contempt relief.
4. It provides direct judicial support for defending the **punitive costs** outcome as a response to repeated urgent litigation that could not be substantiated.
5. It gives the appeal layer a firm anchor: the **30 March 2026** leave-to-appeal notice must now be read against what the judgment actually said.
