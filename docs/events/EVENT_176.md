## Description
Hon. Justice Labuschagne handed down judgment and order in Case 2025/137857 on 26 March 2026 in the contempt proceedings arising from the 19 August 2025 interdict. The later Notice of Application for Leave to Appeal makes clear that Peter Andrew Faucitt sought to appeal **the whole judgment and order**, which strongly implies that the contempt application was not granted in the form sought and that punitive costs were awarded against the applicant.

The grounds recorded in the appeal notice show that the judgment turned on several decisive issues: the relationship between the **Fakie** contempt framework and **Plascon-Evans**, the interpretation of clause 2.5 of the 19 August 2025 order, whether stock-release and training-related incidents amounted to prohibited interference, whether the respondent's version was "far-fetched and untenable," and whether punitive costs were justified.
