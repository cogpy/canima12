## Internal Pleading Instability Revealed by the Document

The affidavit materially strengthens the chronology, but it also reveals a pleading weakness that must be tracked carefully in all future filing work.

### Caption-body respondent mismatch

On the opening caption pages, the respondent mapping is presented as:

- **Third Respondent:** Regima Worldwide Distribution (Pty) Ltd
- **Fourth Respondent:** Regima Skin Treatments CC
- **Fifth Respondent:** Villa Via Arcadia No 2 CC
- **Sixth Respondent:** Strategic Logistics CC

However, in paragraphs **2.3** and **2.4** of the body, the affidavit reverses the third/fourth respondent identification:

- **2.3** describes the **third respondent** as **Regima Skin Treatments CC**
- **2.4** describes the **fourth respondent** as **Regima Worldwide Distribution (Pty) Ltd**

This is not a trivial drafting issue. It creates a respondent-number instability inside the same affidavit and makes it unsafe to rely on bare ordinal references such as "third respondent" or "fourth respondent" without repeating the legal name and registration number.

## Significance

1. This event extends the March 2026 chronology beyond the contempt/appeal layer by showing that a **parallel Part B delinquency-director strategy** was also active in the same period.
