# March 2026 Contempt-to-Appeal Cluster

**Cluster window:** 2026-02-09 to 2026-05-04
**Anchor events:** [EVENT_176](../events/EVENT_176.md), [EVENT_177](../events/EVENT_177.md), [EVENT_179](../events/EVENT_179.md)
**Primary direct sources:** `evidence_documents/LABUSCHAGNE_CONTEMPT_JUDGMENT_2026-03-26.pdf`; `docs/evidence/court/judgments/139F-faucitt-v-faucitt-leave-to-appeal-judgment-2026-05-04.pdf`

## Purpose

This relation note captures the procedural chain that links the **09 February 2026 contempt application**, the **26 March 2026 dismissal with punitive costs**, the **30 March 2026 leave-to-appeal notice**, and the **4 May 2026 leave-to-appeal judgment**. The direct judgment shows that this cluster is not merely a routine appellate step. It is a **weaponised process sequence** in which disputed stock and training incidents were pressed into a contempt framework and then immediately escalated after failure.

## Why This Cluster Matters

1. It ties **February-March 2026 process conduct** back to the broader pattern of procedural leverage already visible in the August 2025 interdict cluster.
2. It shows that the court did not accept the applicant's practical-control theory as self-proving contempt.
3. It gives a direct judicial basis for arguing that the March 2026 sequence is part of a larger **control / pressure / litigation leverage** pattern.

## Cycle 10 Conduct-Contrast Layer

The 4 May 2026 judgment converts this relation cluster from an open appellate bridge into a closed judicial micro-cycle. The Cycle 10 Alexander transformation therefore adds a **conduct-contrast schedule**: each applicant pressure point should now be paired with the court's outcome language and a burden-specific boundary.

| Pressure point | Judgment-anchored contrast | Filing consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Clause 2.5 was used as if it excluded Mrs Faucitt from business involvement. | The judgment records that clause 2.5 prohibits prejudicial conduct and does not preclude involvement in the businesses as such. | Use as a governance-correction point, especially in High Court and CIPC materials. |
| Stock and training incidents were framed as contempt. | Breach and wilfulness were not established; the respondent version was accepted on motion-proceedings principles. | Treat contempt and leave-to-appeal issues as procedurally closed, while keeping criminal allegations source-document driven. |
| Prior affidavits were recycled into later urgent relief. | The judgment records that relevance to the later urgent application was not demonstrated. | Use as a procedural-leverage and affidavit-discipline point. |
