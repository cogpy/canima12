# Supplementary Founding Affidavit Rebuttal Addendum (2026-04-21)

**Applies to:** Any filing, affidavit response, or GitHub Pages note that uses the **16 March 2026** supplementary founding affidavit integrated as [EVENT_178](../events/EVENT_178.md).  
**Purpose:** Defeat the strongest predictable attacks arising from the affidavit by using tightly limited, demonstrable propositions only.

## Executive Summary

The supplementary founding affidavit should be used as a **controlled evidentiary layer**, not as a shortcut. Its strongest value is that it directly proves the applicant's **March 2026 litigation posture**: a sworn **Part B** delinquency-director / probation application existed on **16 March 2026**, and the applicant relied on an order marked **PF36** and a forensic report marked **PF37**. Its main weakness is that the affidavit itself contains an internal **RWD/RST respondent-number reversal**.

That weakness is not fatal if the response papers handle it correctly. The proper answer is not to deny the document. The proper answer is to **use the document carefully**, identify its own instability expressly, and then move to **legal-name + registration-number precision**.

## Rebuttal Layer 1: Neutralise the respondent-number attack through explicit normalization

### Preferred formulation

> The supplementary founding affidavit is used here with entity normalization. Although the caption identifies Regima Worldwide Distribution (Pty) Ltd as the third respondent and Regima Skin Treatments CC as the fourth respondent, paragraphs 2.3 and 2.4 reverse those ordinal labels. For that reason, this response uses the legal company names and registration numbers rather than relying on bare ordinal references.

### Why this defeats the attack

It turns the internal defect into a reason for **greater precision**, rather than allowing the defence to weaponise the ambiguity.

## Rebuttal Layer 2: Limit the affidavit to what it directly proves

### Safe propositions

| Proposition | Safe to assert? | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| A Part B supplementary founding affidavit was sworn on 16 March 2026 | Yes | Directly proved by the jurat |
| The applicant sought delinquency-director / probation relief | Yes | Directly pleaded in paragraph 4.2 |
| The applicant relied on PF36 and PF37 | Yes | Directly pleaded in paragraphs 3.3 and 5.1 |
| PF37's underlying accounting conclusions are established by this affidavit alone | No | The annexure itself must still be proved |
| The R10,847,862.00 figure is independently verified by this affidavit alone | No | The figure is pleaded, not independently validated here |

### Preferred formulation

> This affidavit is direct evidence of what the applicant alleged on 16 March 2026 and of the litigation posture then adopted. It is not, by itself, final proof of every accounting proposition said to appear in PF37.

## Rebuttal Layer 3: Defeat the methodology attack on the R10,847,862.00 figure

### Preferred formulation

> The pleaded figure of R10,847,862.00 is treated here as an allegation advanced in the applicant's supplementary affidavit. Any final accounting conclusion must remain anchored to the underlying forensic material, ledger support, and independent financial records rather than to the pleading alone.

### Why this works

This prevents overreach and blocks the defence from claiming that one disputed accounting number collapses the entire case model.

## Rebuttal Layer 4: Separate the Part B layer from the contempt layer

### Preferred formulation

> The 16 March 2026 supplementary affidavit demonstrates a contemporaneous Part B delinquency-director / probation strategy. The 26 March 2026 judgment addresses the separate contempt application. The chronology therefore records parallel March 2026 litigation activity, not a single undifferentiated process step.

### Why this defeats the attack

It prevents the opponent from portraying the repository as collapsing distinct proceedings into one rhetorical bundle.

## Rebuttal Layer 5: Use the affidavit as a chronology anchor, not as a universal proof engine

The affidavit now properly supports the following propositions across the filing families:

1. **Civil / response architecture:** the applicant escalated to a sworn Part B delinquency case by 16 March 2026.
2. **CIPC / Companies Act context:** the applicant himself cast the dispute in director-delinquency / probation terms.
3. **Commercial-crime / tax context:** the applicant was expressly relying on a forensic-expense narrative, but the repository preserves the distinction between pleaded allegation and independently proved accounting fact.
4. **Timeline coherence:** the affidavit fills the evidentiary gap between the 13 March 2026 Rule 30/30A deadline and the 26 March 2026 contempt judgment.

## Bottom line

The best response is disciplined restraint. Use the affidavit to prove **date**, **litigation posture**, **annexure reliance**, and **internal numbering instability**. Do not use it to overclaim final accounting truth. That approach defeats the strongest red-team attacks while preserving the document's real evidentiary value.
