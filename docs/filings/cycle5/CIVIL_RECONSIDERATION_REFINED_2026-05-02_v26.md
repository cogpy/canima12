## 2. Grounds for Reconsideration (Rule 6(12)(c))

### 2.1 Material Non-Disclosure on the *Ex Parte* Application

Material facts within the applicant's own knowledge were not placed before Khumalo J on 19 August 2025:

| Material Fact Withheld | Why It Was Material | Anchor |
|---|---|---|
| Daniel held the sole banking mandate | Defeats the urgency premise of "unauthorised payments" | Anchor 2 |
| Bantjies was a Trustee of the Faucitt Family Trust at the time he swore the supporting affidavit | Defeats the "independent" representation of the deponent | Anchor 4 |
| Peter cannot read or operate digital documents | Goes to the validity of his signature on the founding affidavit | Anchor 1 |
| The Sage system had been deliberately migrated by Rynette to deny all parties access to financial records | Defeats the "obstruction by Daniel" narrative | Anchor 3 |
| A settlement agreement had been signed two days before the application | Demonstrates that the *ex parte* mechanism was a tactical sequel, not an emergency | Cycle 2 evidence |

A court that knew these five facts could not, on the *Plascon-Evans* test, have granted the order in the form granted.

### 2.2 The Continuing-Harm Trigger

Reconsideration under Rule 6(12)(c) is not limited to defects existing at grant. Where the operation of the order itself produces continuing prejudice that the court did not foresee, the court retains jurisdiction to reconsider. Annexure A demonstrates that R2,850,000 was extracted from RWD between 26 August and 11 September 2025 (the immediate post-grant window), and that the RegimA SA operating balance moved from R2.5M+ to R5,284 in the period October 2025 — January 2026.

## 3. Grounds for Rescission (Rule 42(1)(a) and Common Law *Void Ab Initio*)

The order was erroneously granted in the absence of the respondent on the basis of an affidavit that contained material non-disclosure, perjury, and identity displacement amounting to fraud on the court. The order is therefore *void ab initio* and falls to be rescinded under Rule 42(1)(a) and the common law.

The applicable test (Lodhi 2 Properties; Colyn v Tiger Food Industries) is satisfied because each of the five anchors above was within the applicant's exclusive knowledge and was nevertheless not placed before the court.

## 4. Grounds for Rule 30 Strike-Out of the 16 March 2026 Supplementary Affidavit

### 4.1 The Caption-Body Respondent Inversion

The supplementary founding affidavit filed by the applicant on 16 March 2026 (EVENT_178) reverses the identities of the third and fourth respondents between the caption pages and the body of the affidavit. Specifically:

- **Caption pages:** Third Respondent = Regima Worldwide Distribution (Pty) Ltd 2011/005722/07 [RWD]; Fourth Respondent = RegimA Skin Treatments CC 1992/005371/23 [RST].
- **Body paragraphs 2.3 and 2.4:** Third Respondent described with attributes of RST; Fourth Respondent described with attributes of RWD.

The applicant therefore cannot identify which entity it accuses of director delinquency under Section 162 of the Companies Act 71 of 2008. The s.162 relief is unsustainable on the papers and the affidavit is an irregular step within Rule 30(1).

### 4.2 The R10,847,862 "Computer Expenses" Allegation Lacks an Identifiable Subject

Because the s.162 relief targets a respondent whose identity is unstable inside the applicant's own pleading, the R10,847,862 quantum cannot be tied to any specific entity's accounts.
