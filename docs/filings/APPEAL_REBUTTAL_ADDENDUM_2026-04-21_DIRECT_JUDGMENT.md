## Strategic Position

The strongest respondent-side answer is now straightforward: the appellate notice must be read against the **actual judgment**, and the actual judgment records that the applicant **failed to prove breach and wilfulness**, that the respondent-side version was supported by affidavits, and that punitive costs were justified in light of repeated urgent litigation that could not be substantiated.

The response should therefore resist any drift back into abstraction. The issue is not whether the applicant can state doctrinal propositions about *Fakie*, *Plascon-Evans*, clause 2.5, or costs. The issue is whether the applicant can show that the court was wrong on the **real paper record** before it.

## Response Upgrades to Preserve

## 1. Anchor the answer in the court's actual findings

The response should say, in substance:

> The appeal notice seeks to re-characterize the March 2026 outcome as if it turned on abstract legal methodology alone. The direct judgment shows otherwise. The court accepted that the order existed and that knowledge was present, but held that the applicant failed to prove the decisive remaining elements: breach and wilfulness.

## 2. Separate business friction from contempt

The response should insist that disputed stock control, training attendance, certificate authority, and warehouse interaction do not automatically become contempt merely because the applicant re-labels them as "practical control" or "dealing with the business."

> The judgment makes clear that the applicant's evidence did not convert these contested operational incidents into proof of wilful defiance.

## 3. Use the direct stock findings aggressively

The response should not leave the stock issues at the level of generality. It should expressly invoke the court's finding that the evidence did not indicate disruption of stocktake or delivery by the first respondent.

That is powerful because it defeats the applicant's tendency to treat logistics disagreement as self-proving contempt.

## 4. Use the direct punitive-costs reasoning aggressively

The court recorded that the applicant had approached the court on numerous occasions on an urgent basis for relief that could not be substantiated, and that there was substance in the concern that contempt proceedings were being used to bolster the forfeiture claim in the divorce.

> The response should frame the costs issue as a product of chronology, not as an isolated sanction.

## 5. Keep the void-*ab-initio* bridge alive without overclaiming

The response should say that the appeal already fails on the direct March 2026 record, but that the applicant's position is further weakened because the underlying order remains attacked on integrity grounds.

> Even without deciding the void-*ab-initio* theme, the direct judgment already shows that the applicant did not prove contempt on the papers.
