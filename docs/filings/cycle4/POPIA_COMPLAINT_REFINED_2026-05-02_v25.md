This complaint is filed under POPIA s.74. The contraventions detailed are not historical; they are continuing, present-tense breaches of statutory duty under POPIA s.8 (Conditions for Lawful Processing), s.10 (Minimality), s.13 (Purpose Limitation), s.14 (Further Processing), s.19 (Security Safeguards), and s.20 (Information Processed by Operator). Criminal referral is sought under POPIA Chapter 11 (s.100–107).

The defence of *authorised access* is anticipated and pre-empted in Section 4 below.

## 3. Statutory Contraventions

### 3.1 Section 8 — Conditions for Lawful Processing

The Responsible Parties did not give effect to the eight conditions for lawful processing during the period 28 June 2024 — present. In particular, the Operator (Rynette Farrar) processed personal information of customers, suppliers, and the deponent Peter Faucitt without lawful authority, in breach of s.8(b) (purpose specification) and s.8(c) (further processing limitation).

### 3.2 Section 10 — Minimality

The retention by the Operator of administrative credentials for the `pete@regima.com` account, and the use of those credentials to swear affidavits, file SARS returns, and conduct correspondence in the persona of Peter Faucitt, exceeded what was adequate, relevant, and not excessive given the purpose for which the credentials were originally entrusted.

### 3.3 Section 13 — Purpose Limitation

Customer banking-instruction personal information was processed for a purpose (revenue diversion to third-party accounts) other than the purpose for which it was originally collected (legitimate billing of customers of RWD and RST).

### 3.4 Section 14 — Further Processing

The migration of the `pete@regima.com` Sage account, evidenced by Anchor 1, constitutes further processing not compatible with the purpose for which the personal information was originally collected.

### 3.5 Section 19 — Security Safeguards

The Responsible Parties failed to maintain the integrity and confidentiality of personal information. Specifically, the deliberate expiry of the Sage subscription on 23 July 2025 (foreknowledge_register entry: Rynette, 2025-07-23) destroyed accounting records constituting personal information of customers, suppliers, and employees.

### 3.6 Section 20 — Information Processed by Operator

The Responsible Parties did not have written agreements with the Operators (Rynette Farrar; Daniel Jacobus Bantjies) governing the processing of personal information, in breach of s.21(1).

## 4. Anticipated Defence: "Authorised Access"

The defence will argue that Rynette Farrar, as bookkeeper, had authorised access to the Responsible Parties' systems and that the dispute is therefore a managerial-authorisation dispute, not a POPIA criminal matter.

The defence fails because POPIA distinguishes **mere access** from **purpose-limited processing**. Authorised access becomes unlawful processing the moment access is used for:

| Use | Anchor |
|---|---|
| Impersonation or credential misuse | Anchor 1 (Sage user switch) |
| Payment redirection to new banking details | Anchor 4 (banking-detail-change cluster) |
| Post-exposure retaliation and concealment | EVENT_011 → EVENT_012 sequence (June 2025) |
| Exclusion of the rightful business operators | EVENT_FNB001, EVENT_FNB002 (FNB independent correspondence) |
