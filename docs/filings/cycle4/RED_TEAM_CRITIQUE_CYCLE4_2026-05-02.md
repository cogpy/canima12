**Reviewer perspective:** A senior commercial defence advocate retained by the applicant.

---

## Purpose

This document tests the cycle 4 filing set against the strongest defence arguments now available to the opposing party. The function of the document is **internal stress-testing**, not advocacy. Each attack is followed by the prosecution counter-rebuttal already embedded in the cycle 4 filings.

---

## Attack 1 — "PILLAR X is a back-door for relitigating the contempt application"

**Defence argument.** Labuschagne J's judgment of 26 March 2026 dealt with five identified incidents and found that wilfulness was not established. Cycle 4 attempts to revive the same complaint by re-packaging it as "continuing prejudice."

**Prosecution counter.** PILLAR X expressly **does not** revisit the five incidents adjudicated by Labuschagne J. The Continuing Prejudice Schedule excludes the Mphande stocktake, the Cherie Smith training session, and the other three incidents identified in that judgment. The Schedule is built from independently-evidenced post-grant transactions (FNB statements; Shopify invoices; Sage admissions; the Jacqui FA reconsideration "no income since June 2025" finding) that were not before Labuschagne J.

**Status:** Defence attack defeated.

---

## Attack 2 — "Quantum is inflated"

**Defence argument.** The R10,847,862 line in Annexure A is sourced from the applicant's own supplementary affidavit. The respondent cannot rely on the applicant's own contested allegation as if it were proved.

**Prosecution counter.** PILLAR X uses the R10,847,862 figure **against** the applicant: the line item is captioned *"Mar 2026 — RWD/RST (instability) — Disputed 'computer' expenses — 10,847,862 — EVENT_178 caption-body defect"*. The defect is not that the figure is true but that the supplementary affidavit cannot identify which respondent it accuses. The cycle 4 filings cite the figure as evidence of pleading instability, not as proven loss.

**Status:** Defence attack defeated by careful captioning.

---

## Attack 3 — "Authorised access defence revives"

**Defence argument.** Rynette Farrar had administrative authority to manage Sage; what she did with that authority is a managerial dispute, not a POPIA criminal matter.

**Prosecution counter.** The POPIA filing distinguishes mere access from purpose-limited processing. Cycle 4 strengthens the distinction with the Domain-Substitution Anchor (Anchor 2 of POPIA filing), which evidences not access to existing systems but the establishment of an alternative system (`regimaskin.co.za`) for the purpose of redirecting customer payments. Authorised access cannot extend to creating a parallel processing infrastructure for a competing purpose.

**Status:** Defence attack neutralised.

---

## Attack 4 — "Threshold statements are mere drafting flourish"

**Defence argument.** Each cycle 4 filing opens with a threshold statement and an independent-evidence-anchors section. These add length without adding substance.

**Prosecution counter.** The threshold statement performs a specific procedural function: it pre-empts the regulator's most common refusal ground (wrong forum, wrong burden, historical grievance). The independent-evidence-anchors section pre-empts the most common defence (deponent self-authorisation). Both are responsive to the v22 red-team critique's Loophole 5 (procedural overload) and were absent from earlier cycles.

**Status:** Defence attack does not survive comparison with v22 critique.

---

## Attack 5 — "Caption-body defect is pedantic"

**Defence argument.** Court papers contain typographical errors all the time. A cross-reference between the third and fourth respondent in the supplementary founding affidavit is correctable on the papers.

**Prosecution counter.** The cycle 4 Civil Reconsideration brings the defect under Rule 30(1) — irregular proceedings — within the 10-day window. If the applicant elects to amend, it must explain to the court how a R10,847,862 quantum was attached to a respondent whose identity it could not stabilise inside its own affidavit. If the applicant elects not to amend, the affidavit (and therefore the s.162 relief) falls.

**Status:** Defence attack converts into a tactical dilemma for the applicant.

---

## Attack 6 — "Foreknowledge convergence lattice (Pillar XII candidate) is conspiracy theory"

**Defence argument.** Concentrating control actions in the 22-month window before the Ketoni payout is post-hoc pattern-matching. Many things happened in that window for many reasons.

**Prosecution counter.** Cycle 4 expressly does **not** instantiate Pillar XII; it remains a candidate latent center for Cycle 5 pending independent corroboration (Ketoni board minutes; George Group correspondence). The cycle 4 filings cite the Ketoni timeline only in the NPA Commercial Crime referral and only as motive context, consistent with the v22 critique's instruction to keep Ketoni as motive amplifier rather than sole proof.

**Status:** Defence attack mitigated by deliberate restraint in cycle 4 scope.
