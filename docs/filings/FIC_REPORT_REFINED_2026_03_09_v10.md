## 3. Executive Summary of Suspicious Activities

This report details a large-scale money laundering operation involving the proceeds of crime from fraud, theft, and tax evasion, with a total quantum exceeding **R63,000,000**. The suspects have used a network of at least eight ABSA bank accounts and a dual corporate identity to launder the funds from a hijacked R8.5M/year revenue stream.

The accountable institutions and individuals involved, including the suspects themselves, have failed to report these suspicious transactions as required by the Financial Intelligence Centre Act (FICA). This report is submitted to rectify this failure and trigger an immediate investigation.

## 4. Grounds for Suspicion (Section 29 of FICA)

### 4.1 Laundering the Proceeds of the Revenue Hijacking Chain

-   **The Activity:** Over R10.27M in revenue was diverted from legitimate FNB accounts to a network of at least eight ABSA accounts. This constitutes the laundering of the proceeds of fraud and theft.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Revenue Hijacking Chain](../relations/REVENUE_HIJACKING_CHAIN.md), [EVENT_121](../events/EVENT_121.md)
-   **Suspicion:** The use of multiple, previously unknown accounts to receive legitimate business revenue is a classic indicator of money laundering (layering and integration).

### 4.2 Use of a Dual Corporate Identity to Obscure Funds

-   **The Activity:** The suspects operate a dual corporate identity (Villa Via / Kaylovest Three) under a single CIPC registration. This structure is used to obscure the ultimate beneficial ownership of assets and funds, a key red flag for money laundering.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Dual Corporate Identity](../relations/DUAL_CORPORATE_IDENTITY.md), [EVENT_124](../events/EVENT_124.md)
-   **Suspicion:** The complex and opaque corporate structure has no apparent legitimate business purpose and is likely intended to conceal the proceeds of crime.

### 4.3 Failure to Report by an Accountable Institution

-   **The Activity:** Daniel Bantjies, as the accountant for the entities and a registered tax practitioner, is an accountable institution under FICA. He was aware of the fraudulent inter-company invoices, the revenue diversion, and the suspicious corporate structures, yet failed to file a single Suspicious Transaction Report (STR).
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_119](../events/EVENT_119.md) (admission of manufacturing answers for SARS)
-   **Suspicion:** The failure to report, combined with his active participation in the underlying crimes, indicates a willful conspiracy to commit and conceal money laundering.

## 5. Request for Investigation

We request that the FIC urgently investigate these suspicious activities, including:
1.  The ultimate beneficial ownership of the eight+ ABSA accounts.
2.  The flow of funds from the hijacked revenue stream.
3.  The failure of Daniel Bantjies and other involved accountable institutions to comply with their reporting obligations under FICA.
