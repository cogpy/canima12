## 3. Executive Summary of Tax Fraud

This report details a deliberate scheme to commit tax fraud by falsifying accounting records and misrepresenting the nature of financial transactions. The suspects, an accountant and a company director, have conspired to evade tax liabilities through a series of fraudulent maneuvers.

**New evidence from the Ketoni Investment** reveals a critical instance of this fraud. A R9.8 million investment made by the Faucitt Family Trust was intentionally misallocated on the company's books as a personal loan from a director. This has significant tax implications, as it fraudulently alters the tax liabilities of both the trust and the individual.

Furthermore, the accountant, Daniel Bantjies, has been caught on email admitting his intent to **"manufacture"** answers for a SARS query, providing direct proof of a conspiracy to defraud the revenue service.

## 4. Specific Criminal Acts (Tax Fraud)

### 4.1 Fraudulent Misallocation of Investment for Tax Evasion
-   **The Crime:** On 9 May 2023, accountant Daniel Bantjies instructed that a R9.8 million investment from the Faucitt Family Trust into Ketoni Investment Holdings be recorded as a personal loan from director Peter Faucitt. This fraudulent accounting entry was designed to:
    1.  Evade donations tax that would be applicable if the funds were treated as a gift.
    2.  Create a fictitious loan liability on the company's books.
    3.  Allow Peter Faucitt to potentially draw funds from the company tax-free as a loan repayment.
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_KET_006](../events/EVENT_KET_006.md), [EVIDENCE: KET-06](../evidence/ketoni_evidence_analysis.md)
-   **Violation:** Income Tax Act, 58 of 1962; Tax Administration Act, 28 of 2011.

### 4.2 Conspiracy to Defraud SARS
-   **The Crime:** On 6 June 2025, in response to a SARS query, Daniel Bantjies emailed Rynette Farrar stating, **"I will manufacture an answer to the 2 interco invoices over the weekend."** This is a direct admission of a conspiracy to mislead and defraud the South African Revenue Service by providing false information.
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_119](../events/EVENT_119.md)
-   **Violation:** Tax Administration Act, 28 of 2011 (s234 - Unprofessional Conduct, s235 - Criminal Offences).

## 5. Request for Prosecution

The evidence presented is clear and incontrovertible. It shows a deliberate and calculated effort to defraud the fiscus. We urge the NPA to pursue criminal prosecution against the suspects for tax fraud, conspiracy, and related offenses.
