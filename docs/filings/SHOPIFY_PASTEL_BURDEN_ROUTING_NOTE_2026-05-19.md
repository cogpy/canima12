# Shopify/Pastel Burden Routing Note — Cycle 15

**Date:** 2026-05-19  
**Primary event:** [EVENT_009 — Shopify Order Rerouting to Pastel](../events/EVENT_009.md)  
**Related relation:** [SHOPIFY_PASTEL_ROUTING_2026_05_19](../relations/SHOPIFY_PASTEL_ROUTING_2026_05_19.md)  
**Purpose:** Provide filing-safe language for the 22 May 2025 Shopify/Pastel evidence center.

This note standardizes how the corrected Shopify/Pastel event should be used across the filing stack. The live formulation is that Shopify orders were **rerouted to Pastel** or otherwise concealed through a Pastel/Sage accounting pathway, causing **practical audit-trail severance** and revenue-visibility collapse. Older language referring to “deletion” or “destruction” should be read as describing practical evidentiary impairment unless platform logs later prove literal deletion.[1] [2]

## Filing-Safe Formulations

| Forum | Burden / Use Standard | Recommended Wording | Avoid Unless Separately Proved |
|---|---|---|---|
| Civil action and urgent relief | Balance of probabilities / interim relief | “The order stream was rerouted to Pastel or made inaccessible from the legitimate Shopify audit path, impairing revenue oversight.” | “All Shopify orders were deleted.” |
| CIPC Companies Act complaint | Regulatory/probity threshold | “The rerouting created an undisclosed accounting path and impaired directors’ access to company revenue records.” | “Directors destroyed the entire e-commerce database.” |
| POPIA criminal complaint | Data processing and access-control threshold | “Customer/order data appear to have been processed through an undisclosed or inaccessible accounting route, requiring information-officer and operator disclosure.” | “Personal information was permanently destroyed.” |
| Commercial Crime submission | Investigation trigger / reasonable suspicion | “The rerouting requires preservation and production of Shopify, Pastel/Sage, email, user-access, bank, and VAT records.” | “Deletion is already conclusively proved.” |
| NPA and SARS tax-fraud referral | Criminal and tax reconciliation threshold | “The revenue stream and VAT/accounting trail require reconciliation between Shopify, Pastel/Sage, bank accounts, and SARS/VAT returns.” | “Tax fraud is proved solely by deletion.” |
| SAICA / professional misconduct | Professional conduct and accounting integrity | “The accounting pathway requires explanation, working papers, and reconciliation because practical audit continuity was severed.” | “The accountant personally deleted Shopify records” unless logs show that actor-level fact. |

## Evidence Requests to Attach or Seek

The corrected formulation strengthens discovery and subpoena requests because it identifies the systems that must be reconciled rather than relying on an unsupported deletion theory. Filing drafts should request or annex the following evidence where available.

| Evidence Category | Target System / Custodian | Legal Function |
|---|---|---|
| Shopify order exports | Shopify / account owner | Determine visible order count, deletions, cancellations, export logs, and access history. |
| Pastel/Sage customer and invoice exports | Pastel/Sage / accountant / administrator | Determine whether Shopify-origin orders or customer records were routed into accounting outside the legitimate Shopify audit path. |
| User and access logs | Shopify, Pastel/Sage, email, cloud admin | Identify actor-level access, export, deletion, and permission changes. |
| Bank statements and merchant settlements | Banks / payment processors | Reconcile order revenue against bank deposits and customer receipts. |
| VAT/SARS records | SARS / tax practitioners | Reconcile declared turnover and VAT treatment against actual order revenue. |
| Email instructions | Exchange/Gmail mailboxes | Establish who instructed rerouting, account changes, concealment, or disclosure refusal. |

## Pleading Discipline

The central pleading distinction is between **effect** and **mechanism**. The current evidence supports the effect: order visibility and revenue-audit continuity were severed. The precise mechanism — rerouting, concealment, export, deletion, or mixed conduct — should be resolved from platform and accounting logs.

> **Recommended sentence:** “The evidence presently supports practical audit-trail severance through Shopify-to-Pastel rerouting; literal deletion remains a forensic question to be determined from platform logs and accounting exports.”

This formulation keeps civil and regulatory pleadings strong while preserving criminal-threshold integrity. It also prevents opposing parties from defeating the point by producing partial surviving Shopify records. Partial survival of records would not answer the central allegation if the revenue path was rerouted, concealed, or made inaccessible to the legitimate directors.[1] [2]
