## 3. Offences

### 3.1 TAA s.234 — False Statement

The 2019 fabricated financial statements (Anchor 4) and the SARS-flagged inter-company invoices (Anchor 1) constitute false statements made in dealings with SARS, an offence under s.234.

### 3.2 TAA s.235 — Tax Evasion

The R5.4M stock adjustment (Anchor 2) processed without restatement materially understated the taxable income of Strategic Logistics CC for the 2025 year of assessment, with associated VAT consequences. Tax evasion under s.235 is established by the wilful intent demonstrated in the Bantjies admission of "huge gaps... building up over many years" coupled with the decision to "permanently remove this Bernadine gogga" rather than restate.

### 3.3 TAA s.29 — Duty to Keep Records

The deliberate expiry of the Sage subscription (Anchor 3) breaches the s.29 duty to keep records for 5 years. This is a continuing offence: each day the records remain inaccessible is a fresh breach.

### 3.4 VATA — Output Tax Misallocation

The inter-company invoice cluster (Anchor 1) misstated output and input VAT across the SLG → RST → RWD chain. Where transfer pricing manipulation produces VAT understatement at the consolidated level, criminal liability attaches under VATA s.58.

### 3.5 POCA s.2 — Pattern of Racketeering

The five anchors above, occurring over a period of more than four years (2021 — 2026), establish the "pattern of racketeering activity" required by POCA s.2. The "enterprise" is the consolidated control structure operated by Rynette Farrar and Daniel Jacobus Bantjies under the apparent authority of Peter Andrew Faucitt.
