## Strategic Position

The notice for leave to appeal should be answered, not with rhetorical overreach, but with a **record-discipline response**. The decisive respondent-side advantage is that the appellate notice already narrows the disputed field to a manageable set of issues:

1. the civil-contempt standard;
2. the role of *Plascon-Evans* in motion proceedings;
3. the meaning of clause 2.5;
4. stock and training allegations;
5. witness treatment;
6. punitive costs; and
7. the unresolved challenge to the integrity of the underlying order.

## Response Upgrades Required

## 1. Reframe the contempt question around **clarity + wilfulness + proof**

The answer should insist that contempt is not made out merely because a party participated in business-adjacent activity after an order. The applicant must still prove:

- what the order clearly prohibited,
- what the respondent in fact did,
- why that conduct fell inside the prohibition,
- why the conduct was wilful and mala fide, and
- why any competing explanation does not raise a reasonable doubt.

### Response language to preserve

> The applicant's notice collapses several distinct enquiries into one conclusion. A disputed stock-control or training interaction does not become contempt merely by being redescribed as "practical control." The enquiry remains whether a clear order was knowingly and wilfully defied on proven facts.

## 2. Force precision on each factual incident

Every stock or training allegation should be converted into a separate proof cell:

| Incident | Exact date/time | Documentary source | Alleged act | Alternative explanation | Proven prejudice? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Stock release episode 1 | TBD | Email / stock sheet / affidavit | Alleged delay | Operational disagreement / stock control issue | TBD |
| Training session episode 1 | TBD | Email / witness / schedule | Alleged interference | Founder attendance / authority dispute | TBD |

### Why this matters

When incidents are atomized, broad contempt rhetoric often loses force because each event must independently satisfy the standard.

## 3. Use the appeal notice itself as an admission of weakness in the first-instance outcome

The notice proves:

- the applicant lost on 26 March 2026 in material respects,
- punitive costs were imposed,
- the same stock/training theory remains central, and
- the applicant still depends on attacking the court's method rather than demonstrating incontrovertible facts.

### Response language to preserve

> The appeal notice demonstrates not that contempt was proved, but that the applicant remains dissatisfied with a fact-sensitive judicial rejection of his theory. Its repeated invocation of broad principles cannot substitute for documentary certainty on the incidents relied upon.

## 4. Keep the void-*ab-initio* bridge alive

The response should refuse to let the matter be compartmentalized into a narrow contempt-only frame. If the 19 August 2025 order was procured through material non-disclosure, false premises, or foreknowledge of contradictory facts, that context remains crucial.

### Response language to preserve

> The appellate complaints cannot be assessed in a vacuum. A contempt framework built atop an order attacked as void or fraudulently procured requires heightened caution, not expansive inference.

## 5. Neutralize the witness attack by over-corroboration

The notice hints that the Mphande affidavits were wrongly relied upon. The answer should not depend on them alone. It should cross-link:

- delivery records,
- stock sheets,
- training schedules,
- emails contemporaneous to the incidents,
- attorney correspondence,
- warehouse/phone/body-guard sequence records.

### Response language to preserve

> Even if one strips the matter of witness-character disputes, the applicant still fails to bridge the evidential gap between contested business interaction and proven wilful contempt.

## 6. Turn punitive costs into a chronology issue

The response should show how the litigation escalated through repeated urgent and contempt framing despite persistent underlying disputes. A short chronology should be attached:

- 19 Aug 2025 — interdict granted;
- 30 Sep 2025 — contempt threat over training attendance;
- 4 Feb 2026 — contempt application launched;
- 25 Feb 2026 — supplementary contempt/urgent material analyzed;
- 26 Mar 2026 — judgment/order against applicant;
- 30 Mar 2026 — leave-to-appeal notice filed.

### Response language to preserve

> The punitive-costs complaint is unsustainable if the applicant's own litigation chronology reveals repeated attempts to convert disputed operational friction into quasi-penal relief.
