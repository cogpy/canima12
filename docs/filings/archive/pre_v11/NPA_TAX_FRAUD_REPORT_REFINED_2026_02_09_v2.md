## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report details evidence of significant tax fraud committed by **Peter Andrew Faucitt**, **Rynette Farrar**, and the complicit accounting firm **De Novo Business Services (Pty) Ltd**, represented by **Marisca Meyer**. The fraud involves the deliberate fabrication of financial records, the creation of fictitious expenses to reduce tax liability, and the misrepresentation of the company’s financial position to the South African Revenue Service (SARS).

These acts were not isolated errors but part of a wider criminal conspiracy to defraud the RegimA group of companies. The perpetrators’ acquisition of a **void ab initio (in terms of Uniform Rule 42(1)(a) for being erroneously sought and granted in the absence of the affected party)** High Court interdict (Case 2025-137857) was a key part of their strategy, allowing them to seize control and manipulate financial records without oversight.

## 1. THE PERPETRATORS

-   **Peter Andrew Faucitt:** Director who authorized and benefited from the fraud.
-   **Rynette Farrar:** Financial controller who orchestrated the fraudulent accounting entries.
-   **De Novo Business Services / Marisca Meyer:** The "professional" accountants who knowingly created and signed off on backdated, fraudulent financial statements.

## 2. THE FRAUDULENT SCHEME

The scheme had two primary components designed to defraud SARS and the company.

### 2.1. Fabrication of Financial Statements

-   **The Act:** On **25 June 2025**, De Novo Business Services, on the instruction of Rynette Farrar, issued financial statements for RegimA SA (Pty) Ltd for the 15-month period ending **28 February 2019**.
-   **The Fraud:** These statements were **backdated by over six years**. They were not a reflection of historical reality but a fabrication created in 2025 to create a false paper trail. This constitutes uttering and a direct attempt to mislead SARS.

### 2.2. Creation of Fictitious Loan Accounts & Expenses

-   **The Instruction:** In emails dated 17-18 June 2025, Rynette Farrar gave explicit instructions to De Novo to create a fictitious loan account for another company, Rezonance, and to post expenses belonging to other entities onto it.

> "No, I think the best will be to create a loan account within RegimA SA, for the other company. Then these invoice (not belonging to RegimA SA) can be allocated to this loan account."

-   **The Purpose:** This was a clear instruction to commit tax fraud by:
    1.  Inflating the expenses of RegimA SA with costs it did not incur, thereby illegally reducing its taxable income.
    2.  Creating a false liability on the books of another company (Rezonance) without the knowledge or consent of its director.

## 3. THE ROLE OF THE VOID AB INITIO INTERDICT

The fraudulent interdict obtained on 19 August 2025 was critical to the success of the tax fraud. By illegally removing the oversight of the other directors (Jacqueline and Daniel Faucitt), Peter Faucitt and Rynette Farrar were able to:

-   **Prevent Scrutiny:** Stop the legitimate directors from questioning the fraudulent accounting entries.
-   **Control the Narrative:** Ensure that only the fabricated records were available for any potential audit.
-   **Consolidate Control:** Use the court order to legitimize their sole control over the financial and administrative arms of the companies, allowing them to continue their fraudulent activities unimpeded.
