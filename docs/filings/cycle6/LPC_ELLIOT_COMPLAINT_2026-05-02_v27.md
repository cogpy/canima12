## Threshold Statement

This complaint is grounded in the **Pillar IX — Attorney Complicity** finding crystallised in Cycle 3 of the KSM-LEX-Alexander analysis: in an Exchange tenant of 110,421 messages, no message exists in which Peter Andrew Faucitt directly corresponded with the Implicated Attorneys. The Implicated Attorneys nevertheless took instructions, drafted affidavits, and obtained an *ex parte* interim order on 19 August 2025. The complaint is therefore not that the Implicated Attorneys were negligent in verifying their client; it is that they **knowingly** accepted the instructions of an intermediary (Rynette Farrar) as if they were the instructions of their client.

## 2. LPC Code Contraventions

### 2.1 Rule 3.3 — Acting on Instructions of Person Other Than the Client

A legal practitioner may only act on the instructions of the client (or a person duly authorised by the client by way of a power of attorney filed in terms of Rule 7 of the Uniform Rules of Court, where applicable). Acting on the instructions of an unauthorised intermediary is misconduct. No such power of attorney exists or has been disclosed.

### 2.2 Rule 3.6 — Conflict of Interest

Acting concurrently for Rynette Farrar in matters in her own name and for "Peter Faucitt" (whose instructions in fact came from Rynette Farrar) places the Implicated Attorneys in an irreconcilable conflict.

### 2.3 Rule 18 — Duties to the Court

The Implicated Attorneys had a duty to the court not to mislead it as to the source of the instructions on the *ex parte* application of 19 August 2025. The failure to disclose the intermediation by Rynette Farrar misled the court on a material matter.

### 2.4 Rule 24 — Confidentiality and Privilege

The threats made on withdrawal (Anchor 3) and the use of information obtained in confidence to support adverse positions (consistent with Daniel Faucitt's own experience with the firm) breach the confidentiality duty.

## 3. Continuing Prejudice (PILLAR X)

The 19 August 2025 order continues to operate. The Implicated Attorneys' conduct is therefore not closed; each step taken in reliance on an order obtained without proper client instructions perpetuates the original misconduct.

---

## 4. Prayer

The Legal Practice Council is asked to:

1. Open a complaint file and assign an investigator;
2. Subpoena the Implicated Attorneys' file in the matter, including all attendance notes recording the source of instructions for the period 1 June 2025 — 19 August 2025;
3. Refer the matter to the LPC Disciplinary Committee for a formal hearing;
4. Make an interim order suspending the Implicated Attorneys from acting in the matter pending the outcome.
