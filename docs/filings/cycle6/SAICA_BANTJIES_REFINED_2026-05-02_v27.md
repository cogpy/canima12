## 3. Code of Professional Conduct Contraventions

### 3.1 Section 100 — Fundamental Principles

Bantjies has contravened the principles of:

- **Integrity** (s.110): the 46-second forward (Anchor 1) is direct evidence of a deliberate misrepresentation to the directors;
- **Objectivity** (s.120): the triple conflict (Anchor 4) prevents objective professional judgment;
- **Professional Competence and Due Care** (s.130): the failure to detect the R5.4M stock discrepancy across multiple years, then attempting to "permanently remove" the issue rather than restate, breaches due care;
- **Confidentiality** (s.140): the unauthorised forward of director-level communications to the bookkeeper breaches confidentiality;
- **Professional Behaviour** (s.150): swearing a confirmatory affidavit without disclosing trusteeship brings the profession into disrepute.

### 3.2 Section 200 — Threats and Safeguards

The trusteeship of the FFT and the CFO role at The George Group create **self-interest**, **self-review**, **familiarity**, and **intimidation** threats simultaneously. No safeguards exist that could reduce these threats to an acceptable level. The only compliant action would have been to resign from the engagement; Bantjies did not resign.

### 3.3 NOCLAR (Sections 225, 360 of the Code)

Bantjies became aware of multiple instances of non-compliance with laws and regulations (including Companies Act, Tax Administration Act, and POPIA breaches) and failed to discharge his NOCLAR obligations: he did not communicate with the directors charged with governance, did not consider resignation, and did not consider disclosure to an appropriate authority.
