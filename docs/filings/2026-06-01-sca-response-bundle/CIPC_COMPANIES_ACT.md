## CoR135.1-style complaint narrative

The complainants request that CIPC evaluate this complaint under the Companies Act complaint route. CIPC states that a complaint relating to an alleged contravention of the Companies Act may be reported by completing Form CoR135.1 and sending it to the CIPC complaints address.[19] This document is drafted as the narrative annexure to that form and should be attached to the completed CoR135.1 filing.

## Complainant and standing

The complainants are affected by the governance, authority and company-record issues described in this complaint. The formal capacity should be selected on the CoR135.1 form after attorney verification, including whether the complaint is lodged as shareholder, director, former director, employee, creditor, data subject, affected person or authorised representative of an affected entity.

## Companies Act issues for CIPC evaluation

| Issue | Companies Act governance concern | Documents to attach |
|---|---|---|
| Director authority and fiduciary duties | Whether directors or officers acted for proper purpose, in good faith and in the best interests of the company. | Resolutions, mandates, correspondence and board records. |
| Related-party transactions | Whether rent, expense allocation, e-commerce costs, intercompany charges or benefits were properly authorised and disclosed. | Ledgers, invoices, management accounts and related-party schedules. |
| Records and access | Whether statutory records, accounting records, registers, resolutions or company information were withheld, altered or selectively used. | Requests for records, responses, registers and accounting extracts. |
| Oppressive or prejudicial conduct | Whether company conduct unfairly prejudiced shareholders, directors or affected stakeholders. | Chronology, correspondence, litigation records and financial-flow evidence. |
| Litigation use of company structures | Whether company processes were used to advance private disputes or economic-control objectives. | Court papers, authority documents and event records. |

## Facts relied on

The evidence presently indicates that company and group structures may have been used in a manner that obscured entity-specific revenues, expenses, authority and benefits. The complaint also raises whether directors, officers, accountants or controllers caused companies to carry costs or risks for the benefit of other persons or entities, and whether litigation steps were based on an incomplete description of company authority and economic control.

## Relief and action requested from CIPC

The complainants request that CIPC acknowledge the CoR135.1 complaint, evaluate jurisdiction, appoint inspectors or require information where appropriate, require production of statutory and accounting records, consider compliance notices, and refer any criminal or regulatory issues to the appropriate authority. The complainants also request written reasons if CIPC declines jurisdiction or refers the matter elsewhere.
