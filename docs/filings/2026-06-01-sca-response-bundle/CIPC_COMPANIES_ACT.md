## Consolidated source lineage

This v33 complaint aggregates the v32 CIPC filing with the canonical v21 Companies Act complaint and the evidence-enhanced historical CIPC draft. The consolidated version expands the earlier high-level governance issues into statutory heads concerning records, financial statements, false statements, fiduciary duties, improper purpose and oppressive or prejudicial conduct.[12] [16]

## Purpose

This is a complete governance complaint. It requests investigation of company-record, director-conduct, mandate, authority, operational-control, financial-reporting and information-access issues arising from the dispute and related court proceedings. The complaint is framed for CIPC or another Companies Act forum because the record appears to raise corporate-governance issues that cannot be fully resolved by contempt or appellate litigation alone.[16]

## Summary

The evidence record indicates a dispute over who was entitled to act for, manage, access or control company operations and records. The foundation order and later contempt/SCA steps are relevant because they appear to have been used to affect or reinforce governance outcomes before the underlying company-authority disputes were fully ventilated.[1] [2] [3]

## Relationship to civil proceedings

This complaint does not ask CIPC to set aside the High Court order. That is the function of the Rule 42/common-law rescission application. CIPC’s concern is narrower and corporate: whether the underlying company records, director conduct, accounting records and governance acts complied with the Companies Act and whether corrective action, compliance notices or referral are warranted.[8] [12] [16]

## Requested relief / action

The complainants request that CIPC or the appropriate Companies Act forum review the attached evidence; require production of company records, resolutions, registers and financial records; verify director and mandate history; investigate potential director misconduct, false statements, improper purpose or oppressive conduct; direct corrective steps where records or governance actions are defective; and consider referral for prosecution or delinquency-related relief where statutory thresholds are met.[16]

## Evidence to attach

The complaint should be filed with company registration documents, director records, resolutions, bank mandates, correspondence, accounting records, stock schedules, the foundation order, the contempt judgment, the SCA petition transcript and a chronology of operational-control events. If the complaint is routed through the Companies Tribunal rather than CIPC, the relief section should be adapted to the prescribed procedure.

Before filing, each entity should be identified by registered name, registration number, director list, affected bank accounts and relevant tax/VAT number where applicable.
