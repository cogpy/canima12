## Purpose

This is a complete draft governance complaint. It requests investigation of company-record, director-conduct, mandate, authority, operational-control and information-access issues arising from the dispute and related court proceedings.

## Summary

The evidence record indicates a dispute over who was entitled to act for, manage, access or control company operations and records. The foundation order and later contempt/SCA steps are relevant because they appear to have been used to affect or reinforce governance outcomes before the underlying company-authority disputes were fully ventilated.[1] [2] [3]

## Companies Act issues for investigation

| Issue | CIPC / Companies Act relevance |
|---|---|
| Director authority | Whether directors or persons acting as directors exceeded or misrepresented authority. |
| Company records | Whether company records, resolutions, mandates or registers accurately reflect lawful authority. |
| Fiduciary duties | Whether any director or prescribed officer acted contrary to company interests or for improper purpose. |
| Oppressive or prejudicial conduct | Whether governance steps unfairly prejudiced stakeholders or affected persons. |
| Mandates and banking authority | Whether corporate authority was properly granted, revoked, recorded and communicated. |

## Requested relief / action

The complainants request that CIPC or the appropriate Companies Act forum review the attached evidence, require production of company records and resolutions, verify director and mandate history, investigate potential director misconduct or improper purpose, and direct corrective steps where records or governance actions are defective.

## Evidence to attach

The complaint should be filed with company registration documents, director records, resolutions, bank mandates, correspondence, the foundation order, the contempt judgment, the SCA petition transcript, and a chronology of operational-control events.
