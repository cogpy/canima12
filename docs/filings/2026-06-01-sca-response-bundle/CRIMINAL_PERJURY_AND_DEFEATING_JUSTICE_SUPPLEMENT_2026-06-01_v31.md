# Criminal Referral Supplement — Perjury, Fraud-on-the-Court, and Defeating the Ends of Justice — v31

**Author:** Manus AI  
**Date:** 1 June 2026  
**Matter:** High Court case number 2025/137857 and related SCA petition  
**Filing route:** Prosecutorial / police referral supplement; not a substitute for a sworn complainant statement

> **Threshold warning.** This supplement is drafted for evidentiary routing. It must not state final guilt. It should identify prima facie facts, preserve the chain of custody, and request investigation where direct evidence supports a reasonable suspicion.

## 1. Purpose of this supplement

The 1 June 2026 SCA petition is relevant to the criminal track because it shows continued reliance on the 19 August 2025 interim order. If that order was obtained through perjury, material non-disclosure, or fraud on the court, continued invocation of the order may be relevant to motive, persistence, and ongoing prejudice. The petition is not, by itself, proof of a criminal offence. It is a procedural event that should be cross-referenced to the underlying proof of alleged false statements, foreknowledge, and materiality.[1] [2] [3] [4]

## 2. Criminal theory kept within proof limits

The criminal theory should be framed conditionally and evidentially. The core allegation is that certain founding or supporting evidence used to obtain the ex parte order may have been knowingly false or materially incomplete, and that the resulting order was then used as a litigation instrument. The current SCA petition matters because it seeks to revive contempt proceedings based on that order. It does not eliminate the need to prove each false statement, the maker’s knowledge, materiality, causation, and prejudice through admissible primary documents.[2] [4]

## 3. Evidence map for investigators

| Issue | What the SCA petition adds | What independent evidence must prove | Investigative request |
|---|---|---|---|
| Continued reliance | The applicant asks the SCA to revive contempt proceedings founded on the August order. | That the August order was procured by false or materially incomplete evidence. | Obtain ex parte papers, full court file, and service record. |
| Foreknowledge | The petition demonstrates persistence after dismissal and refusal of leave. | Documents showing the applicant or supporting witnesses knew the contrary facts before or when affidavits were made. | Secure email, banking, mandate, and attorney correspondence metadata. |
| Materiality | The petition emphasises paragraph 2.5 as operationally decisive. | That the ex parte court would likely have approached relief differently had omitted facts been disclosed. | Compare ex parte allegations to banking mandates and company records. |
| Prejudice | The petition attempts to keep the order operative against Jacqueline. | Actual business, legal, financial, and reputational prejudice caused by the order and its invocation. | Obtain court orders, operational records, and financial chronology. |

## 4. Recommended wording for complaint update

The complainant should state that the SCA petition is supplied as a further procedural exhibit showing that the contested August order continues to be relied upon as a live instrument. The complainant should not ask investigators to infer guilt merely from the act of petitioning the SCA. Instead, the request should be that investigators examine whether the persistence of reliance, when read with the primary evidence of alleged falsehoods and omissions, supports investigation into perjury, fraud on the court, and defeating or obstructing the administration of justice.[2] [4]
