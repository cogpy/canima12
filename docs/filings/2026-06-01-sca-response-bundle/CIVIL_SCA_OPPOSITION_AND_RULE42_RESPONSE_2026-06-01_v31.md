## 1. Civil response theory

The 1 June 2026 petition should be answered as a live appellate step, not as background evidence. The applicant seeks SCA leave after the High Court refused leave on 4 May 2026. The petition attacks the 26 March 2026 contempt judgment and order, particularly the interpretation of paragraph 2.5 of the 19 August 2025 interim order and the High Court’s handling of the *Fakie* contempt burden-shift and motion-proceeding disputes.[1] [2] [5] [6]

The civil response should therefore proceed on two coordinated lines. The first line is defensive: oppose leave because there is no reasonable prospect that another court would overturn the contempt dismissal, and no compelling reason why a fact-bound urgent contempt dispute belongs before the SCA. The second line is structural: accelerate rescission or a declaration of voidness because the petition confirms that the applicant continues to invoke the August order as a control instrument. That continuing reliance strengthens the practical necessity of attacking the foundation order rather than only defending individual contempt episodes.[2] [4]

## 2. SCA opposition paragraphs for immediate settlement

The respondent should deny that the petition discloses a section 17 basis for SCA intervention. The application remains fact-bound. It asks an appellate court to re-open disputes about stock, training attendance, certification authority, employee interaction, and a work phone, where the High Court found that the applicant did not establish contempt beyond reasonable doubt and where respondent-side explanations were supported by third-party affidavits.[1] [2] [6]

The respondent should further plead that *Fakie* does not assist the applicant unless non-compliance is first proved. The applicant must prove a clear order, knowledge, non-compliance, wilfulness, and *mala fides*. Where the alleged breach itself depends on contested interpretation and contested operational facts, the respondent’s version and supporting affidavits remain relevant under motion-proceeding principles. The quasi-criminal character of contempt makes ambiguity fatal to committal-type relief.[6] [7]

The punitive-costs issue should be separated from the contempt merits. If the applicant’s strongest point is that attorney-and-client costs were insufficiently particularised, that does not justify leave against the entire contempt dismissal. The respondent should defend costs by annexing the pattern of urgent proceedings, failed relief, and the use of contempt proceedings in a wider control dispute. If the evidentiary record is not strong enough to defend every aspect of the punitive order, the fallback position should be that any costs reconsideration is severable and does not create a reasonable prospect on contempt merits.[2]

## 3. Rule 42 and common-law rescission update

The petition confirms that the August order is not spent. It is being used as the legal premise for continued appellate litigation. That fact should be pleaded in the rescission papers as a new procedural event showing ongoing prejudice and practical urgency. The rescission application should remain strictly evidence-facing: it must identify the exact ex parte statements alleged to be false or materially incomplete, the primary documents proving the contrary, and why the omitted or misstated facts would probably have affected the ex parte court’s decision.[2] [4]

The revised rescission papers should not read like a criminal charge sheet. They should ask the civil court to decide whether the order was erroneously granted in the absence of affected parties, whether material facts were withheld, whether fraud or perjury vitiated the order, and whether the continuing invocation of the order justifies rescission, variation, or declaratory relief. Criminal referrals may follow, but the civil rescission document should remain focused on jurisdiction, materiality, prejudice, and relief.[4]

## 5. Proposed relief posture

For the SCA petition, the response should seek dismissal of the petition with costs, alternatively dismissal of the merits petition while allowing any costs issue to be dealt with separately if the SCA considers that necessary. For the High Court rescission route, the draft should seek rescission or variation of the August order, declaratory relief on voidness where properly supported, appropriate costs, and referral of objectively false statements or prima facie offences to competent authorities only where the primary evidence is complete.[2] [4]
