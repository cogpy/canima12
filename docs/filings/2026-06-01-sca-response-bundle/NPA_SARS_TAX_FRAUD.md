## Consolidated source lineage

This v33 request aggregates the v32 SARS/NPA filing with the comprehensive 2025 tax-fraud report and the canonical v21 SARS Tax Administration Act skeleton. The consolidated version keeps the more detailed historical categories—under-declaration, false records, stock/invoice discrepancies, transfer or intercompany treatment and economic benefit—while treating them as matters for SARS audit and prosecutorial screening rather than final determinations.[11] [18]

## Purpose

This is a complete request for investigation of possible tax, undeclared-benefit, revenue-stream, intercompany, stock-valuation and economic-benefit issues arising from the broader dispute. It is drafted as an investigative request because tax liability, criminal tax intent and entity-level treatment must be determined from source records held by SARS, banks, accountants, companies and relevant third parties.[18]

## Summary

The civil litigation record raises questions about who controlled or benefited from business revenue streams, bank accounts, company resources, stock values, invoices and intercompany allocations. If any person or entity diverted income, concealed receipts, mischaracterised payments, created false invoices, misstated stock, shifted taxable benefit or caused inaccurate returns, SARS and the NPA are the proper bodies to investigate.[11] [18]

## Issues for SARS/NPA evaluation

| Issue | Investigation required | Records to obtain |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue control and beneficial receipt | Identify bank accounts receiving revenue and persons controlling withdrawals or transfers. | Bank statements, merchant records, payment-gateway records and management accounts. |
| Under-declaration or diversion | Determine whether income was omitted, redirected or reported by the wrong taxpayer. | Tax returns, VAT returns, ledgers, sales reports and bank reconciliations. |
| False invoices or intercompany charges | Determine whether invoices reflected real services and correct entities. | Source invoices, contracts, delivery evidence and intercompany journals. |
| Stock and inventory valuation | Determine whether stock discrepancies affected taxable income, VAT, customs or financial statements. | Stock counts, warehouse records, inventory adjustments and audit files. |
| Economic benefit to individuals | Determine whether personal benefits, withdrawals, use of assets or debt relief were taxed correctly. | Loan accounts, payroll, director accounts, expense claims and related-party records. |
| Litigation-linked economic advantage | Determine whether court process assisted concealment, control or continued misreporting. | Court papers, affidavits, SCA petition and attorney correspondence. |

## Tax Administration Act screening heads

| Statutory issue | Threshold-safe framing |
|---|---|
| False statements or records | SARS should determine whether any return, invoice, ledger, stock record or explanation was false or misleading in a material respect.[18] |
| Tax evasion or attempted evasion | SARS should determine whether any conduct was intentional and had the effect of evading tax, VAT or penalties.[18] |
| Obstruction or defeating administration | SARS should determine whether records were withheld, altered or routed through litigation to impede accurate tax administration.[18] |
| Reportable irregularities | If accountants, directors or tax practitioners were involved, SARS should consider whether professional or regulatory reporting duties arise. |

## Relationship to the SCA petition

The SCA petition is not itself a tax document. Its relevance is chronological and evidentiary: it shows continued reliance on a litigation position tied to operational and commercial control. SARS should therefore treat it as a context document, not as tax proof. The tax investigation must rest on bank, accounting, invoice, return, stock and beneficial-ownership records.[1] [2] [3] [11]

## Requested action

The complainants request that SARS obtain bank statements, tax returns, VAT submissions, accounting ledgers, invoices, payroll records, shareholder/director records, stock records, intercompany transfer records, court papers and correspondence; conduct a risk review and audit where warranted; preserve original electronic records; assess whether criminal tax offences or recoverable under-declarations are present; and coordinate with the NPA if intentional evasion, fraud or obstruction is supported by evidence.
