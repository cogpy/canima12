## Tax-risk referral letter

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| To | SARS Criminal Investigations / SARS compliance enforcement / National Prosecuting Authority tax or commercial-crime channel |
| From | Daniel Faucitt / affected complainants or authorised representatives |
| Subject | Request for tax-risk, understatement, economic-benefit and related-party transaction screening |
| Filing posture | Tax-risk referral and request for investigation, not a completed tax assessment or final audit conclusion |

Dear Sir / Madam,

This referral asks SARS and, where appropriate, the NPA to screen the attached evidence for possible tax offences, understatement risk, unreported benefits, misallocated expenses, related-party extraction, VAT or income-tax consequences, and economic benefits arising from disputed company-control and litigation conduct. The complainants do not purport to make a final tax assessment. The request is for SARS to obtain and test the source records.

## Entities and flows requiring SARS/NPA screening

| Screening area | Core question | Records requested |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue allocation | Were revenues received by the entity entitled to them, or redirected through another person or entity? | Bank statements, merchant-platform records, Shopify records, invoices and customer receipts. |
| Expense allocation | Were expenses booked to entities that did not receive the corresponding benefit? | Ledgers, journals, supplier invoices, intercompany accounts and management accounts. |
| Related-party rent or extraction | Were related-party charges arm’s length, properly authorised and tax accounted for? | Lease records, rent schedules, board approvals and tax computations. |
| Inventory and cost-of-sales adjustments | Were inventory or COS adjustments commercially supported and tax consistent? | Stock records, production records, trial balances and audit workpapers. |
| Litigation-linked economic benefit | Did litigation steps preserve, transfer or conceal economic value? | Court papers, authority records and transaction chronology. |

## Tax-risk heads for investigation

The issues requiring SARS/NPA evaluation include possible understatement of income, improper deduction of expenses, unreported fringe or related-party benefits, VAT consequences of misallocated supplies, PAYE or remuneration questions where payments were characterised inconsistently, and possible common-law or statutory fraud if records were knowingly misstated. These issues are framed as investigative questions because SARS must determine tax liability from the primary accounting and tax records.[18]

## Requested SARS/NPA action

The complainants request that SARS open a risk review or criminal-investigation screening file; obtain relevant tax returns, VAT returns, PAYE records and supporting schedules for the entities and persons identified in the evidence bundle; compare those records with banking and platform-source data; preserve accounting-system audit trails; and consider referral to the NPA if the evidence supports criminal prosecution.
