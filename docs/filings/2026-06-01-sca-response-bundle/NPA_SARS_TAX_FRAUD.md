# Complete Draft Filing — NPA / SARS Tax-Fraud and Economic-Benefit Investigation Request

**Recipients:** SARS Criminal Investigations and, where appropriate, the National Prosecuting Authority  
**Complainants:** Daniel Faucitt and Jacqueline Faucitt  
**Draft date:** 2026-06-01  
**Prepared by:** Manus AI

## Purpose

This is a complete draft request for investigation of possible tax, undeclared-benefit, revenue-stream and economic-benefit issues arising from the broader dispute. It is drafted as an investigative request because tax liability, criminal tax intent and entity-level treatment must be determined from source records held by SARS, banks, accountants and companies.

## Summary

The civil litigation record raises questions about who controlled or benefited from business revenue streams, bank accounts, operational access and company resources. If any person derived taxable benefits, diverted income, concealed receipts, mischaracterised payments, or caused entities to submit inaccurate returns, SARS and the NPA are the proper bodies to investigate.

## Issues for SARS/NPA evaluation

| Issue | Investigation required |
|---|---|
| Revenue control | Identify accounts receiving revenue and persons controlling withdrawals or transfers. |
| Beneficial receipt | Determine whether individuals or entities received economic benefits not properly recorded. |
| Tax declarations | Compare bank records, accounting records and tax submissions. |
| Litigation-linked economic advantage | Determine whether court process was used to secure economic control or conceal benefits. |
| Entity allocations | Determine whether income, expenses and inter-entity transfers were allocated to the correct taxpayer. |

## Relationship to the SCA petition

The SCA petition is not itself a tax document. Its relevance is that it confirms continued reliance on a litigation position tied to operational and commercial control. That makes it a useful chronology anchor for SARS/NPA investigators, but the tax investigation must be grounded in bank, accounting and return data rather than litigation rhetoric.[1] [2] [3]

## Requested action

The complainants request that SARS and, where appropriate, the NPA obtain bank statements, tax returns, accounting ledgers, invoices, payroll records, shareholder/director records, intercompany transfer records and court papers, then determine whether tax offences, reportable irregularities or recoverable under-declarations are present.
