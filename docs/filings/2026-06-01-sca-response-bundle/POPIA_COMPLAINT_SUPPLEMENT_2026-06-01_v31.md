# POPIA Complaint Supplement — Data-Control and Information-Officer Boundary Update after SCA Petition — v31

**Author:** Manus AI  
**Date:** 1 June 2026  
**Forum:** Information Regulator / POPIA complaint and, only where justified, criminal referral  
**Matter:** Data-control and information-governance issues linked to case number 2025/137857

> **Scope note.** POPIA allegations must be tied to specific personal information, responsible parties, operators, processing acts, information-officer duties, and evidence of unlawful processing or obstruction. The SCA petition is not, by itself, proof of a POPIA violation.

## 1. POPIA relevance of the SCA petition

The SCA petition is relevant only if the broad control theory advanced under paragraph 2.5 affected access to personal information systems, client records, employee data, training/certification records, or information-officer functions. The petition refers to employees, client relations, training, certification, and business administration, but a POPIA complaint requires more than those references. It requires evidence of specific data processing, interference, unauthorised access, unlawful disclosure, retention, destruction, or obstruction of statutory responsibilities.[1] [2]

## 2. Updated POPIA posture

The POPIA response should therefore be conservative. It should add the SCA petition as a context exhibit showing that data-bearing operational areas remain contested, while reserving criminal POPIA language for facts that are independently proven. The complaint should identify each affected data category, responsible party, system, processing act, evidence item, and requested remedy.[2]

| POPIA issue | What must be proved | Petition relevance | Filing status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Client personal information | Specific client-data system and alleged unlawful processing. | Petition mentions clientele and business administration. | Context only until data acts are indexed. |
| Employee information | Staff records or instructions affecting employee data. | Petition references employees and directions. | Needs HR/data-system evidence. |
| Training/certification records | Records containing personal or professional information. | Petition relies on training and certification disputes. | Potentially relevant with records proof. |
| Information-officer obstruction | Appointment, duties, access requirements, obstruction acts. | Petition may show operational control dispute. | Requires role and correspondence proof. |

## 3. Recommended POPIA amendment paragraph

The complainant should submit the 1 June 2026 SCA petition as a supplementary context exhibit only. The complaint should state that the petition confirms continued litigation over operational areas that may contain or depend on personal information. The complainant should then identify the specific POPIA contraventions through separate annexures, rather than treating the SCA petition as the contravention itself.[1] [2]

## 4. Evidence QA before escalation

Before any criminal POPIA complaint is escalated, the evidence pack should confirm the responsible party, operator, information officer, data subjects, data category, processing act, security safeguard, breach or obstruction act, dates, and prejudice. Without that matrix, POPIA assertions should remain regulatory concerns rather than criminal allegations.
