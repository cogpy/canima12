## Nature of the dispute

This application is not a disguised appeal against the March 2026 contempt judgment. It is a direct response to the foundation order. The contempt judgment and SCA petition are relevant because they show the consequences of leaving the disputed order uncorrected: the order is being used as a platform for quasi-criminal relief even though the High Court found that breach and wilfulness were not proved.[2] [3] [8]

## Grounds for rescission

### Erroneous grant in the absence of affected parties

The order was granted without the Court having the full operational, banking, mandate and company-governance context. The omission is material because the order is now being interpreted as if it clearly prohibited broad categories of conduct by Jacqueline Faucitt, including conduct said to be ordinary, authorised or reasonably explained.

### Material non-disclosure

In any urgent or ex parte application, the applicant carries a heightened duty to place material adverse facts before the Court. The applicants contend that material facts concerning authority, mandates, banking control, operational involvement and related governance disputes were not fairly disclosed. If those facts had been disclosed, the Court may have refused the order, narrowed it, required notice, or imposed safeguards.

### Common-law fraud-on-the-court ground

The applicants do not ask this Court to make criminal findings in this civil application. The point is procedural and remedial: an order obtained through material falsehood, concealment or misleading presentation should not remain available as a platform for punitive contempt relief. The common law permits rescission where the integrity of the process by which the order was obtained has been vitiated.

### Ambiguity and contempt risk

The SCA petition shows that Peter seeks to read paragraph 2.5 broadly enough to criminalise disputed operational and professional conduct. A court order cannot safely support contempt unless its terms are clear, the alleged breach is proved, and wilfulness and *mala fides* are established. The March 2026 contempt judgment found that those elements were not established on the papers.[2] [3] [8]
