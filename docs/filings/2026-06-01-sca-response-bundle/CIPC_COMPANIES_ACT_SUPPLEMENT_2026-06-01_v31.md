> **Scope note.** This supplement is designed for governance-regulatory use. It should focus on director conduct, statutory records, authority, control of company affairs, and prejudice to companies. Criminal conclusions should be reserved for competent investigative bodies.

## 1. CIPC relevance of the SCA petition

The SCA petition is relevant to the CIPC track because it argues for a broad interpretation of an interim order regulating participation in business administration, employees, partners, clientele, stock, training, and certification. That is directly adjacent to company-governance questions. If the August order was procured by incomplete or false evidence, or if it is being used to interfere with proper director functions and company administration, the issue is suitable for governance-focused regulatory review.[1] [2] [4]

## 2. Updated Companies Act complaint theory

The CIPC complaint should be updated to say that the SCA petition is a new exhibit showing continued reliance on the August order as a business-control instrument. The complaint should avoid asking CIPC to decide contempt or appellate merits. Instead, it should ask CIPC to review whether the underlying conduct and continuing order-reliance reveal governance failures, misuse of company machinery, obstruction of statutory records, improper control of company operations, or conduct inconsistent with fiduciary obligations.[2]

## 3. Recommended CIPC amendment paragraph

The complainant should append the 1 June 2026 SCA petition and analysis report as a governance-context update. The complaint should state that the petition is relied upon not as a finding of wrongdoing, but as evidence that the disputed August order continues to be invoked to regulate business administration. CIPC is requested to consider whether the wider evidence record shows misuse of director powers, improper control of company affairs, prejudice to companies, or failures in statutory record-keeping and governance.[1] [2] [4]

## 4. Annexure refinement

The CIPC pack should add a table that separates each entity, its directors, relevant records, alleged control event, prejudice evidence, and source document. This entity-by-entity separation is essential because a group-wide narrative may be rejected as too diffuse for regulatory action.
