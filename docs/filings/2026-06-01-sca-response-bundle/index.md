## Purpose of this bundle

This folder now contains **complete draft filings** rather than supplements. The previous v31 bundle was useful as a response layer to the 1 June 2026 SCA petition, but it assumed that earlier filings had already been submitted. The present v32 bundle proceeds on the corrected basis that the civil, criminal, NPA, SARS, CIPC and POPIA filings have **not yet been filed** and therefore must stand on their own.

The filing strategy is dual-track. The first track is civil: oppose the SCA petition while advancing Rule 42/common-law rescission of the foundation order. The second track is investigative and regulatory: refer the underlying conduct to the appropriate bodies using threshold-safe language that requests investigation rather than asserting final criminal guilt.[1] [2] [3]

## Filing order recommendation

The recommended immediate order is: **SCA opposition first**, **Rule 42 rescission in parallel**, then **criminal and regulatory referrals after final evidence pagination**. This sequence avoids allowing the SCA petition to proceed undefended while still attacking the root order. It also prevents criminal/regulatory filings from contaminating the civil opposition with language that belongs in investigative forums rather than appellate submissions.[3]
