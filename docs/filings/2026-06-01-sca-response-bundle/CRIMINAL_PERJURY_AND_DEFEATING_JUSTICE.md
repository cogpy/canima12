# Complete Draft Criminal Referral — Perjury and Defeating the Administration of Justice

**Recipient:** SAPS / Commercial Crime / prosecuting authority  
**Complainants:** Daniel Faucitt and Jacqueline Faucitt  
**Primary subject:** Peter Andrew Faucitt and any other person who knowingly made, procured or persisted in materially false sworn evidence  
**Draft date:** 2026-06-01  
**Prepared by:** Manus AI

## Purpose

This document is a complete draft criminal referral. It requests investigation of suspected perjury, false statements under oath, material non-disclosure in court proceedings, fraud on the court and conduct potentially amounting to defeating or obstructing the administration of justice.

The referral is threshold-safe. It does not assert that guilt has already been proved beyond reasonable doubt. It states that the documentary record justifies investigation, preservation of original evidence and prosecutorial assessment.[1] [2] [3]

## Summary of complaint

The complainants contend that court process may have been used to obtain and enforce an order on an incomplete or misleading factual foundation. The concern intensified when the order was later used for contempt proceedings and then for an SCA petition after the High Court found that breach and wilfulness were not proved.[2] [3] [8]

## Suspected offence categories

| Category | Investigative question |
|---|---|
| Perjury / false sworn evidence | Were any sworn statements materially false when made, and did the deponent know them to be false? |
| Defeating the administration of justice | Was court process used or persisted in despite knowledge of contradictory facts? |
| Fraud on the court | Was the court induced to grant or enforce relief on a materially misleading record? |
| Conspiracy or assistance | Did any person knowingly assist, procure or persist in false or incomplete evidence? |

## Material facts requiring investigation

Investigators should compare the sworn application record with banking, mandate, company-governance, operational and correspondence evidence. The key investigative issue is whether material facts were concealed or misrepresented when the foundation order was obtained and when contempt relief was pursued.
