# Complete Draft Filing — POPIA Complaint

**Recipient:** Information Regulator (South Africa)  
**Complainants:** Daniel Faucitt and Jacqueline Faucitt  
**Draft date:** 2026-06-01  
**Prepared by:** Manus AI

## Purpose

This is a complete draft POPIA complaint concerning the collection, use, disclosure, retention or dissemination of personal information, including potentially sensitive medical, family, financial, professional and litigation-linked information.

## Summary

The complainants request that the Information Regulator investigate whether personal information was processed lawfully, minimally, accurately and for a proper purpose in connection with the litigation, court record, business systems, information-officer obligations and related correspondence. The complaint is fact-sensitive and should be supported by specific examples of personal-information use, disclosure and retention.

## POPIA issues for investigation

| Issue | Investigation required |
|---|---|
| Lawful processing | Determine whether personal information was processed on a lawful basis and for a proper purpose. |
| Minimality | Determine whether more personal or medical information was used than was necessary for the stated purpose. |
| Accuracy | Determine whether inaccurate or misleading personal information was placed in circulation. |
| Security safeguards | Determine whether access to personal data, business systems or litigation records was adequately controlled. |
| Information officer compliance | Determine whether responsible parties had appointed and functioning information officers and complaint procedures. |
| Further processing | Determine whether court-record or business-system information was repurposed beyond its lawful purpose. |

## Relationship to court proceedings

This complaint does not ask the Information Regulator to decide the merits of the SCA petition or the rescission application. The court proceedings are relevant because they are the mechanism through which personal information may have been processed, disclosed or relied upon. The POPIA issue is whether that processing complied with POPIA's conditions for lawful processing.

## Requested action

The complainants request that the Information Regulator open a complaint, require the relevant responsible parties to identify their information officers, preserve and disclose processing records, investigate the use of personal and sensitive information in litigation and business systems, and issue enforcement or corrective directions if non-compliance is established.
