## Consolidated source lineage

This v33 POPIA complaint aggregates the v32 POPIA filing with the detailed 2025 enhanced POPIA draft, the canonical v21 POPIA criminal complaint and the later cycle-9 POPIA forum template. The consolidated version retains the richer factual categories from the older draft while using the later statutory framing around responsible parties, operators, information-officer duties, safeguards, unauthorised access and possible Chapter 11 referral.[13] [17]

## Purpose

This is a complete POPIA complaint concerning the collection, use, disclosure, retention, access, security and further processing of personal information, including potentially sensitive medical, family, financial, professional, business-system and litigation-linked information. It asks the Information Regulator to investigate compliance and, if appropriate, issue enforcement or referral directions.[17]

## Summary

The complainants request that the Information Regulator investigate whether personal information was processed lawfully, minimally, accurately, securely and for a proper purpose in connection with litigation, court records, business systems, email monitoring, information-officer obligations, banking data and related correspondence. The complaint is fact-sensitive and should be supported by specific examples of personal-information use, disclosure and retention.[13] [17]

## POPIA issues for investigation

| Issue | Investigation required | POPIA relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Lawful processing | Determine whether personal information was processed on a lawful basis and for a proper purpose. | Conditions for lawful processing and accountability.[17] |
| Minimality and purpose limitation | Determine whether more personal, financial, medical or family information was used than necessary for the stated purpose. | Processing limitation and purpose specification.[17] |
| Accuracy | Determine whether inaccurate or misleading personal information was placed in circulation, including in legal papers. | Information quality and prejudice. |
| Security safeguards | Determine whether access to personal data, business systems, email, banking records or litigation records was adequately controlled. | Security safeguards and operator control.[17] |
| Information officer compliance | Determine whether responsible parties appointed, empowered and used functioning information officers and complaint procedures. | Accountability and internal governance. |
| Further processing | Determine whether court-record, business-system or banking information was repurposed beyond its lawful purpose. | Further-processing compatibility.[17] |
| Unauthorised access or interference | Determine whether anyone accessed, monitored, copied, redirected or interfered with personal information or systems without authority. | Possible enforcement or Chapter 11 referral.[17] |

## Relationship to court proceedings

This complaint does not ask the Information Regulator to decide the merits of the SCA petition or the rescission application. The court proceedings are relevant because they are a mechanism through which personal information may have been processed, disclosed or relied upon. The POPIA issue is whether that processing complied with POPIA’s lawful-processing, minimality, security, accuracy, retention and accountability requirements.[1] [2] [3] [17]

## Specific factual clusters to assess

| Cluster | POPIA question | Evidence to attach |
|---|---|---|
| Litigation use of personal information | Was personal, medical, family or financial information necessary, accurate and fairly presented in court materials? | Court papers, annexures, correspondence and redacted comparison schedule. |
| Business-system access | Were credentials, emails, system logs, customer data or operational records accessed by authorised persons only? | System logs, credential records, email audit trails and user-rights history. |
| Banking and financial data | Was banking or account data processed for a lawful purpose and protected from unauthorised disclosure? | Bank correspondence, mandate records and disclosure chronology. |
| Information-officer governance | Were responsible parties and operators identifiable, and were complaint procedures functional? | Information officer appointments, privacy notices and internal policies. |
| Further processing and retention | Was information originally held for business, legal or compliance purposes repurposed for unrelated pressure or exclusion? | Chronology, message trails and document circulation logs. |

## Requested action

The complainants request that the Information Regulator open a complaint; require the relevant responsible parties to identify their information officers and operators; preserve and disclose processing records; investigate the use of personal and sensitive information in litigation and business systems; direct a forensic audit of access logs where warranted; require corrective or enforcement steps; and consider referral for criminal investigation if Chapter 11 thresholds are met.[17]
