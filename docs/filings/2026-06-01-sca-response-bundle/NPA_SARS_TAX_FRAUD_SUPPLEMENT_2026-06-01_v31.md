# NPA / SARS Tax Fraud Supplement — Boundary-Control Update after SCA Petition — v31

**Author:** Manus AI  
**Date:** 1 June 2026  
**Forum:** NPA / SARS Criminal Investigations routing  
**Matter:** Financial evidence programme associated with case number 2025/137857

> **Scope note.** The 1 June 2026 SCA petition should not be used as proof of tax fraud. It is relevant only as a litigation-control event that may affect access to records, governance, and prejudice. Tax allegations must remain grounded in financial records, tax filings, bank statements, ledgers, and independent forensic analysis.

## 1. Tax-track significance

The SCA petition seeks to revive contempt proceedings concerning operational restrictions under the August order. That does not prove any tax offence. Its relevance to the NPA/SARS track is indirect: if the contested order was used to interfere with company control, records, banking access, or financial administration, it may explain record-access obstruction or ongoing prejudice that affects tax-evidence collection. The updated tax referral should therefore use the petition as a boundary-control exhibit, not as a tax-evasion exhibit.[1] [2]

## 2. Evidence separation rule

The tax report must preserve strict separation between litigation facts and tax facts. Litigation facts show the procedural history and control context. Tax facts must show false statements, omitted income, improper deductions, VAT misstatements, false invoices, unauthorised inter-company postings, or similar statutory conduct. Without bank/tax/ledger corroboration, the SCA petition should never be described as evidence of tax fraud.[2]

| Question | Required evidence | Role of SCA petition |
|---|---|---|
| Were tax returns false or misleading? | SARS returns, working papers, invoices, VAT schedules, ledger extracts. | None, except context. |
| Were records obstructed or controlled? | Access logs, correspondence, Sage/Pastel/Shopify records, banking access evidence. | Contextual evidence of disputed control environment. |
| Was there deliberate tax evasion? | Direct records showing knowledge, intent, and false treatment. | No direct proof; may show litigation persistence only. |
| Should SARS/NPA receive the SCA exhibit? | Only as an annexure to explain why record access and control remain live. | Supplementary exhibit. |

## 3. Recommended tax referral amendment

The tax referral should be amended to say that the 1 June 2026 SCA petition is appended for procedural context only. The complainant should explain that the petition shows continued reliance on the disputed August order, which may have practical consequences for access to company records and financial-control systems. The tax allegations themselves should be supported separately by financial evidence schedules and, where possible, an independent forensic accountant’s report.

## 4. Immediate tax-track action list

The next tax-track action is not to expand allegations but to complete the evidence matrix. Each alleged false statement or evasion event should be mapped to a tax period, entity, return, source ledger, bank transaction, invoice, responsible person, and proof of knowledge. The SCA petition should appear only in the chronology column as a litigation-control event.[2]
