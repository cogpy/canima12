## 1. Commercial-crime relevance

The SCA petition is commercially relevant because it seeks to preserve or revive a court-order mechanism that affects control of business operations. The petition’s central argument is that paragraph 2.5 of the August order should be interpreted broadly enough to restrict Jacqueline’s involvement in stock, training, certification, employees, client relations, and business administration. That asserted breadth is relevant to a commercial-crime investigation only if it is connected to independent evidence of business-control seizure, revenue-stream diversion, asset prejudice, or the use of litigation to obtain commercial advantage.[1] [2]

## 2. Updated referral theory

The petition should be treated as a control-instrument exhibit, not as the financial evidence itself.[2] [3] [4]
