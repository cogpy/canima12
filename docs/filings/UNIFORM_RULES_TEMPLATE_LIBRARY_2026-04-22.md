**Date:** 2026-04-22  
**Jurisdictional scope:** High Court of South Africa  
**Purpose:** Practical template library for the Uniform Rules of Court, organized around the rules that most often generate reusable filed instruments rather than purely administrative guidance.[1] [2]

## Purpose

This library is designed as a **practical precedent surface** rather than an abstract digest of all Uniform Rules. Many rules govern conduct, timing, service, evidence, or internal court administration without yielding a clean reusable pleading. The focus here is therefore on rules that ordinarily generate **notices, motions, affidavits, pleadings, applications, or response instruments** that can be adapted for real litigation.[1]

This document should be read together with the accompanying **taxonomy** and **CourtOnline workflow** materials already added to the repository. The library uses posture warnings so that users do not confuse action templates with motion templates, or platform-access documents with substantive court process.[2] [3]

## How to Use This Library

| Use question | How to use the library |
|---|---|
| You know the rule number | Go directly to the template heading for that rule family. |
| You know the litigation stage but not the rule | Use the sequence of sections below: motion proceedings, action pleadings, irregular-step practice, discovery, trial preparation, rescission/review, execution, costs, appeal, and special proceedings. |
| You are using CourtOnline | Use the substantive template here together with the CourtOnline access and filing strategy notes already created in the repository. |
| You are unsure whether a template is appropriate | Read the **When not to use** note under the relevant template before drafting. |

# Part I — Motion Proceedings and Representation

## 1. General Notice of Motion — Rule 6

**Use:** General application proceedings.  
**When not to use:** Do not use for action proceedings commenced by summons.

```text
NOTICE OF MOTION

KINDLY TAKE NOTICE THAT the Applicant hereby applies to the above
Honourable Court on a date to be arranged with the Registrar for an order
in the following terms:

1. [Primary relief].
2. [Ancillary relief].
3. Costs of this application [on the scale of / only in the event of opposition].
4. Further and/or alternative relief.

TAKE NOTICE FURTHER that the accompanying founding affidavit of [DEPONENT]
will be used in support of this application.

TAKE NOTICE FURTHER that the Respondent(s) who intend to oppose this
application are required:

(a) to deliver notice of intention to oppose within [5] court days after
    service hereof, alternatively within the period prescribed by the
    applicable directive / notice; and
(b) thereafter to deliver answering affidavit(s) within [15] court days of
    such notice, or within such time as the Rules, the notice, or a court
    directive may require.

DATED at [PLACE] on this [DAY] day of [MONTH] [YEAR].

__________________________________
[ATTORNEY / APPLICANT IN PERSON]
Address for service: [STREET ADDRESS]
                     [EMAIL]
                     [PHONE]
                     Ref: [REFERENCE]

TO: THE REGISTRAR
AND TO: THE RESPONDENT(S) / THEIR ATTORNEYS
```

## 2. Founding Affidavit Skeleton — Rule 6

**Use:** Substantive support for motion relief.  
**When not to use:** Do not treat the notice of motion as a substitute for proper evidentiary averments.

```text
FOUNDING AFFIDAVIT

I, the undersigned,

[FULL NAME]

do hereby make oath and say:

1. I am an adult [occupation / status] residing at [address].
2. I am the [Applicant / duly authorised representative of the Applicant].
3. The facts herein are, unless the context indicates otherwise, within my
   personal knowledge and are both true and correct.
4. This affidavit is filed in support of the Notice of Motion dated [DATE].

PART A: PARTIES
5. The Applicant is [full description].
6. The First Respondent is [full description].
7. The Second Respondent is [full description].

PART B: JURISDICTION
8. This Court has jurisdiction because [basis].

PART C: FACTUAL CHRONOLOGY
9. On [date], [event].
10. On [date], [event].
11. The relevant chronology is set out more fully as follows: [detail].

PART D: LEGAL BASIS FOR RELIEF
12. The Applicant seeks relief under [rule / statute / common law basis].
13. The requirements for such relief are met because [grounds].

PART E: URGENCY [if applicable]
14. The matter is urgent because [facts].
15. The Applicant cannot obtain substantial redress in due course because [facts].

PART F: PREJUDICE / BALANCE / INTERESTS [as applicable]
16. The prejudice to the Applicant is [detail].
17. The Respondent suffers no irreparable prejudice because [detail].

PART G: RELIEF
18. For these reasons I respectfully pray for the relief set out in the Notice of Motion.

DEPONENT: __________________

THUS SIGNED AND SWORN TO before me at [PLACE] on [DATE], the deponent
having acknowledged that [he/she/they] knows and understands the contents
of this affidavit, has no objection to taking the prescribed oath, and
considers the oath to be binding on [his/her/their] conscience.

COMMISSIONER OF OATHS: __________________
```

## 3. Answering Affidavit Skeleton — Rule 6(5)(d)

**Use:** Respondent's substantive answer in application proceedings.  
**When not to use:** Do not use it in place of a plea in action proceedings.

```text
ANSWERING AFFIDAVIT

I, the undersigned,

[FULL NAME]

do hereby make oath and say:

1. I am the [First / Second] Respondent in this matter.
2. I oppose the relief sought by the Applicant.
3. Save where expressly admitted, every allegation in the founding papers
   inconsistent herewith is denied.

PART A: PRELIMINARY ISSUES
4. At the outset I raise the following preliminary issues:
   4.1 [urgency denied / lack of jurisdiction / non-joinder / authority / etc.]

PART B: RESPONSE TO FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS
5. Ad paragraph 9 thereof:
   5.1 I admit / deny / do not admit the contents.
   5.2 The true position is [detail].
6. Ad paragraph 10 thereof:
   6.1 [response].

PART C: AFFIRMATIVE VERSION
7. The correct chronology is as follows: [detail].

PART D: LEGAL RESPONSE
8. The application falls to be dismissed because [grounds].

PART E: RELIEF
9. I accordingly pray that the application be dismissed with costs,
   alternatively with such order as the Court deems fit.

DEPONENT: __________________
COMMISSIONER OF OATHS: __________________
```

## 9. Combined Summons — Rule 17

**Use:** Action proceedings where particulars of claim accompany the summons.  
**When not to use:** Do not use for motion proceedings.

```text

YOU ARE HEREBY SUMMONED that within 10 court days after service upon you
of this summons, you give notice to the Plaintiff of your intention to
defend this action, and thereafter to deliver your plea within the time
allowed by the Rules, failing which judgment may be granted against you.

PARTICULARS OF CLAIM

1. The Plaintiff is [description].
2. The First Defendant is [description].
3. This Court has jurisdiction because [basis].
4. On or about [date] at [place], the parties concluded [agreement / event].
5. The material terms were [terms].
6. The Defendant breached the agreement / committed the delict by [detail].
7. As a result the Plaintiff suffered damages in the amount of [amount], alternatively seeks [relief].

WHEREFORE the Plaintiff prays for judgment against the Defendant for:
(a) payment of [amount];
(b) interest thereon at [rate];
(c) costs of suit; and
(d) further and/or alternative relief.

DATED at [PLACE] on this [DAY] day of [MONTH] [YEAR].

__________________________________
[PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEYS]
```

## Concluding Note

This library is intentionally broad, but it is not infinite. It covers the **principal reusable template families** that ordinarily arise from the Uniform Rules in High Court civil practice. Where a rule is predominantly definitional, operational, or too specialist to standardize responsibly, the better tool is a workflow note or specialist pack rather than a generic precedent. The safest drafting method remains: **identify the litigation posture first, then select the correct rule family, and only then adapt the template**.[1] [2] [3]
