## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report details a network of suspicious and unusual transactions related to a criminal enterprise engaged in money laundering, tax evasion, and fraud, with a total quantum exceeding R63M. The activities involve the abuse of at least eight ABSA bank accounts, complex inter-company transfers, and the use of identity fraud to control financial systems, all constituting reportable offenses under the Financial Intelligence Centre Act (FICA).

The primary suspects, Peter Andrew Faucitt, Rynette Farrar, and Daniel Jacobus Bantjies, have established and utilized a web of entities to conceal the proceeds of crime, primarily derived from the systematic hijacking of an R8.5M/year revenue stream and other fraudulent activities. The motive is the seizure of an impending ZAR 18.685M payout from Ketoni Investment Holdings.

The failure of the responsible parties and accountable institutions (including the suspects themselves in their capacities as directors and accountants) to file Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) in the face of overwhelming evidence of financial crime is, in itself, a criminal offense under FICA. This report is submitted to rectify that failure and to request an immediate investigation by the FIC.

## 3. GROUNDS FOR SUSPICION (SECTION 29 OF FICA)

### 3.1 Suspicion of Money Laundering (Proceeds of Unlawful Activities)

**Unlawful Activities:** Fraud, Theft, Tax Evasion, Racketeering (POCA).

**Suspicious Transactions:**
1.  **Asset Stripping:** Transfer of over R2.5M from RegimA SA's bank account between Oct 2024 and Mar 2025, leaving it with a balance of R5,284 (a 99.8% reduction).
2.  **Use of Multiple Accounts:** Utilization of at least **eight ABSA bank accounts** to layer and integrate illicit funds, making them difficult to trace.
3.  **Complex Inter-Company Transfers:** Funds were moved between various entities (RegimA SA, RegimA Worldwide Distribution, etc.) without legitimate business justification, serving only to obscure the origin of the funds.

### 3.2 Suspicion of Tax Evasion

**Suspicious Transactions:**
1.  **Concealment of Revenue:** The diversion of an R8.5M annual revenue stream away from the declared entity (RegimA SA) to other accounts to evade corporate income tax and VAT.
2.  **Fraudulent Financials:** The creation of fabricated 2019 financial statements in June 2025 was a direct attempt to mislead SARS and justify the movement of funds.

### 3.3 Transactions with No Apparent Business or Lawful Purpose

**Suspicious Transactions:**
1.  The rapid and near-total depletion of a profitable company's bank account is inconsistent with normal business operations.
2.  The circular movement of funds between the various entities controlled by the suspects has no discernible commercial purpose and points towards a laundering scheme.

### 3.4 Suspicion related to the Offences of Financing of Terrorist and Related Activities

Not applicable based on current evidence.
