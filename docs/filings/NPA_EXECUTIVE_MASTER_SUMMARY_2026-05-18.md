## 3. The Three Pillars of Proof (Beyond Reasonable Doubt)

The evidence in this matter exceeds the criminal burden of proof (~95%). It relies not on witness testimony, but on immutable digital forensics, cryptographic audit trails, and the perpetrators' own written admissions.

### Pillar 1: The Revenue Diversion (The Theft)
On 22 May 2025, Rynette Farrar secretly rerouted the company's Shopify e-commerce orders to her own Pastel accounting system (EVENT_009). This severed the audit trail from the perspective of the legitimate directors, dropping a R1M+ monthly revenue stream to zero overnight. Simultaneously, over 120 emails were sent to clients instructing them to change RegimA's banking details from FNB to an ABSA account controlled by the perpetrators (EVENT_121). 
* **Proof:** Shopify routing logs (JF17), 121+ client emails, ABSA bank statements.

### Pillar 2: The Trust Capture (The Forgery)
To legitimize their control over the company's assets, the perpetrators forged the signature of the founder on critical trust documents. Rynette Farrar systematically executed "pp Peter" forgeries to amend the Faucitt Family Trust deed, unlawfully removing legitimate trustees and installing compliant actors.
* **Proof:** The forged trust deed amendments (SF15), forensic handwriting analysis.

### Pillar 3: The Cover-Up (The Perjury and Collusion)
To conceal the theft, the external accountant, Daniel Bantjies, colluded with Rynette Farrar. On 19 August 2025, Bantjies signed a J417 declaration under oath, swearing to his independence. Exactly 46 seconds later, he secretly forwarded confidential family communications to Rynette Farrar with instructions to conceal their coordination (EVENT_137). This perjury was used to obtain a fraudulent ex parte interdict, paralyzing the victims.
* **Proof:** The J417 sworn declaration, the 46-second email timestamp (EVENT_137), and Bantjies' written admission of "huge gaps" in the financials.
