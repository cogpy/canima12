## Finding 1 — Inter-company Loan Account Manipulation

The existing financial-manipulation summary records that, on **17 August 2021**, Rynette directed Bantjies on which journal entries to make to **"fix" intercompany loan accounts**, including backdating entries to August 2020, with the practical effect that **R109,896.92** allegedly disappeared between Villa Via and RegimA Skin.[3] That is not merely a bookkeeping irregularity. In audit terms, it suggests a breakdown in **journal-entry authorization**, **related-party transaction review**, **cut-off**, and **account-reconciliation control design**.[1] [3]

Under the `fin-audit-za-v2` lens, this maps most strongly to **Stage 2: Relatio Elimination (B2.*)** risk, where inter-company flows are expected to pair cleanly and net properly across entities. A pattern of backdated repairs or unexplained disappearing balances is exactly the type of signal that should trigger asymmetry testing, counterparty matching, and escalation for potential misclassification or disguised extraction.[1]

## Finding 2 — Inventory and Stock-Adjustment Fraud Indicators

The evidence already in the repository describes a **Strategic Logistics** stock-discrepancy environment involving approximately **R5.2M to R5.4M** in suspicious inventory adjustments, including **negative inventory balances** and a year-end pattern inconsistent with ordinary accounting discipline.[3] [6] The financial extraction analysis characterizes this as a manufactured loss environment, while the broader case narrative frames it as a long-running concealment mechanism rather than a routine restatement.[3] [6]

From an audit perspective, negative finished-goods positions, large year-end adjustments, and a control environment in which the same operational actors also influence other related-party accounts collectively indicate a likely failure of **inventory existence**, **valuation**, **cut-off**, **management-review controls**, and **period-end close governance**. On the current record, the likely classification is **material weakness**, because the apparent magnitude is high, the transactions are not isolated, and the adjustment appears capable of producing materially misstated financial results and tax consequences.[1] [3] [6]

## Finding 3 — SARS / VAT Query and Ex Post Explanation-Building

The June 2025 SARS / VAT thread is the clearest current audit flashpoint. The case narrative states that SARS queried **"two big inter company invoices"** pushed through at the end of February 2025, after which Rynette indicated that those invoices had been done on Bantjies' request and that she had no answer.[3] The dedicated Danie 000277 evidence note goes further: in a mailbox item dated **6 June 2025**, Danie Bantjies wrote that he would **"manufacture an answer"** for the two inter-company invoices over the weekend.[4]

A neutral tax-control environment would ordinarily require that material inter-company invoices already be supported by contemporaneous commercial rationale, correct entity mapping, VAT treatment, approval chain, and underlying documentation before SARS asks questions. The wording preserved in 000277 strongly suggests the opposite: that the explanatory narrative was being assembled **after** regulatory scrutiny had already been triggered.[4] [5]

Under `fin-audit-za-v2`, this is not a low-level exception. It indicates probable failure in **tax-compliance review**, **document-retention integrity**, **management representation reliability**, **professional independence**, and **NOCLAR-sensitive escalation**. On a preliminary basis, the likely classification is **material weakness**, with strong downstream implications for the **SARS tax-fraud report**, **SAICA misconduct complaint**, and **commercial-crime referral**.[1] [4] [5]

## Finding 4 — Segregation of Duties and Control Override Environment

The case repositories already reflect a pattern in which Rynette appears to have controlled or influenced **Sage access**, **Pete's email context**, and operational financial execution, while Bantjies appears as **external accountant** yet also as a structurally conflicted insider in related trust and group matters.[2] [7] The oversight-chain analysis explicitly describes a network in which the same small set of actors occupied internal bookkeeping, external accounting, trust, and SARS-adjacent positions without effective independent challenge.[7]

This is a classic **segregation-of-duties** failure. More importantly, it suggests **control override**, because the problem is not only incompatible access but the apparent ability to reframe anomalies, suppress challenge, and respond to regulatory issues from inside overlapping roles. Where role overlap is combined with retaliatory conduct after fraud exposure, the risk moves beyond control deficiency toward **material weakness** and possible intentional misconduct.[1] [2] [7]

## Finding 5 — Treasury Control Failure and Subsequent Extraction Pattern

The March-to-September 2025 chronology reflects a pattern in which Peter sought SARS representative control in March 2025, Daniel exposed fraud on **6 June 2025**, cards were cancelled on **7 June 2025**, and operational accounts were later drained after the interdict process.[5] The same timeline records over **R10.6M** moved from operational accounts into savings accounts in early September 2025.[5]

While that sequence is not by itself a complete treasury test file, it strongly suggests failure in **cash-disbursement governance**, **treasury authorization**, **exception escalation**, **retaliation safeguards**, and **post-incident control stabilization**. In an ordinary audited environment, the exposure of possible fraud should trigger preservation, independent review, and ring-fencing of authority. The chronology instead suggests retaliatory restriction followed by large-scale cash movement.[5] The likely classification is therefore **material weakness**.[1] [5]
