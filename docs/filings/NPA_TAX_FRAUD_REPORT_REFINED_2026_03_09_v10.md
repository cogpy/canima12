## 3. Executive Summary of Offences

This report details a multi-year, multi-million rand tax fraud and evasion scheme perpetrated by the suspects. The scheme involves the fraudulent manipulation of inter-company invoices, the concealment of revenue through a hijacked banking infrastructure, and the use of a dual corporate identity to obscure financial reality from SARS.

The evidence is conclusive and includes a direct admission from the scheme's accountant, Daniel Bantjies, of his intent to **"manufacture an answer"** for a SARS query regarding fraudulent invoices. The suspects have systematically dismantled independent oversight and used their control of the corporate structure to commit tax fraud on a massive scale.

-   **The Crime:** On 6 June 2025, when questioned by SARS about "two big inter company invoices," Bantjies admitted in an email his intent to fabricate an explanation. This is a direct confession to defrauding SARS.
-   **Evidence:** [EVENT_119](../events/EVENT_119.md)
-   **Violation:** Income Tax Act (s104 - Intentional tax evasion), VAT Act (s58 - Evasion of tax).

### 4.2 Revenue Hijacking & Concealment (Income Tax & VAT Fraud)

-   **The Crime:** The suspects hijacked over R10.27M in revenue by redirecting client payments to at least eight undisclosed ABSA bank accounts. This revenue was never declared, resulting in massive income tax and VAT evasion.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Revenue Hijacking Chain](../relations/REVENUE_HIJACKING_CHAIN.md), [EVENT_121](../events/EVENT_121.md)
-   **Violation:** Income Tax Act (s104), VAT Act (s58).

### 4.3 Dual Corporate Identity (Tax Evasion)

-   **The Crime:** The use of a dual corporate identity (Villa Via / Kaylovest Three) under a single CIPC registration allows for the concealment of assets and income, creating a shell structure to evade tax liabilities.
-   **Evidence:** [RELATION: Dual Corporate Identity](../relations/DUAL_CORPORATE_IDENTITY.md), [EVENT_124](../events/EVENT_124.md)
-   **Violation:** Income Tax Act (s104 - Evasion through fraud or misrepresentation).

### 4.4 Exchange Control Violations

-   **The Crime:** In a letter to FNB on 17 June 2025, Peter Faucitt makes admissions regarding exchange control irregularities, which require investigation by the South African Reserve Bank (SARB).
-   **Evidence:** SF10 (Peter's FNB Fraud Letter)
-   **Violation:** Currency and Exchanges Act, Exchange Control Regulations.
