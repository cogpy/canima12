**033-23**

Our review of the computer expenses for the period under examination indicates a material increase compared to prior periods. Based on the financial data assessed, this increase does not correspond with the movement in turnover. No proportional rise in revenue, business activity, or operational scale was identified that would explain or justify the elevated level of computer-related expenditure. Accordingly, the increase appears unrelated to changes in turnover and does not reflect a pattern normally associated with revenue-driven operational growth.

**b) Assessment of Turnover After Discontinuation of Shopify-Related Costs**

We further analysed the turnover for the periods following the cessation of Shopify-related expenses. The financial records show that turnover has remained consistent and has not experienced any substantial decline subsequent to the termination of these expenses. This indicates that the discontinuation of Shopify costs did not have an adverse impact on the revenue-generating capacity of the business during the period reviewed. The last time that Regima Skin transacted on Shopify was on the 26th of May 2025.

Please refer to Annexure A, for missing supplier invoices.

Failure to obtain and retain supplier invoices presents significant compliance and financial risks:

**1. Companies Act Compliance**

The Companies Act 71 of 2008 requires entities to maintain accurate and complete accounting records. Missing documentation increases exposure during audits and regulatory reviews, as incomplete records may be construed as negligence or, in severe cases, fraudulent.

**2. SARS Compliance**

SARS mandates valid supporting documentation for all expenses. Missing invoices may result in:
- Disallowance of expense deductions, impacting taxable income.
- Penalties and interest arising from the underestimation of tax and provisional tax.
- Change in assessed loss position, potentially converting to a taxable position.
- Heightened audit scrutiny, including potential audit flags and reputational risk.

**3. This also increases the risk of:**
- Duplicate payments.
- Fraudulent transactions slipping through.
- High administrative costs for resolving disputes.

In the absence of supplier invoices, we are unable to verify the validity of the expense or confirm whether it represents a legitimate, duplicate, or potentially fraudulent transaction.
