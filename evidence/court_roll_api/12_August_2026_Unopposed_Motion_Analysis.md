## Procedural Context and Rule 6(5) Mechanisms

### The Threat of Default Judgment

Under the Uniform Rules of Court, application proceedings are governed by Rule 6. When an applicant serves a notice of motion, the respondent has a specified period (typically 5 days for notice of opposition, followed by 15 days for the answering affidavit) to respond [1]. 

If a respondent fails to deliver an answering affidavit within the prescribed period, Rule 6(5)(f)(i) empowers the applicant to apply to the registrar to allocate a date for the hearing of the application on the **unopposed roll** [1].

In the present matter, the June 2026 filings analysis (specifically Peter's answering affidavit, paragraphs 24.5 and 24.10) explicitly confirms this posture:
1. Peter notes that **no answering affidavit was ever filed** in the main application.
2. Peter makes a direct **threat of unopposed-roll enrolment**.

The discovery of the 12 August 2026 date confirms that Peter has executed this threat. On 14 May 2026, Peter's attorneys filed a "Notice of Set Down — Unopposed Motion" (Annexure JH15) with the hearing date left blank [2]. The Court Online system has now allocated 12 August 2026 as that date.

### Shift from Urgent Court to Ordinary Motion

The 19 August 2025 interdict and the 17 March 2026 contempt hearing were both enrolled in the Urgent Court. The 12 August 2026 enrolment is on the ordinary Motion roll. This indicates that Peter has abandoned the rules of urgency for this specific relief, opting instead to follow the normal timelines for default judgment.

## Likely Relief Sought: The Part B Delinquency Application

The original notice of motion in case 2025-137857 was bifurcated into Part A (interim interdicts) and Part B (final relief). 

### The Part B Prayers

According to the founding papers (Annexure JF07, CCE20250924_0028), the final relief sought under Part B is:
> "an order declaring the first and second respondents delinquent, alternatively placing them under probation." [3]

This relief is grounded in section 162(5) of the Companies Act 71 of 2008. A declaration of delinquency is a severe statutory remedy. If granted, it disqualifies a person from acting as a director for a minimum of seven years (and potentially for life) [4].

### Why Part B is the Target

The 12 August unopposed motion is almost certainly the Part B delinquency application because:
1. **The Interdict (Part A) is already granted:** The interim order of 19 August 2025 remains in force.
2. **The Contempt Application failed:** Labuschagne J dismissed the contempt application on 26 March 2026, and leave to appeal was refused.
3. **The JH15 Notice:** The set-down notice filed on 14 May explicitly lists "PART B, FIRST APPLICATION, AND SECOND APPLICATION" [2].
4. **The Settlement Agreements:** The "Second Application" likely refers to Peter's attempt to make the 18 September 2025 settlement agreements (PF15/PF16) orders of court (Annexure JH16).

If Peter secures a delinquency order by default, he achieves total corporate decapitation of the respondents without having to prove his case at trial or in opposed motion proceedings. As noted in recent jurisprudence, once the grounds in section 162(5) are established—even by default—a court has no discretion but to declare a director delinquent [4].

## Recommended Actions

The discovery of the 12 August 2026 date provides a crucial window of opportunity. The respondents have approximately eight weeks to neutralize the default judgment threat.

### Immediate Term (Days 1–7)

1. **File Notice of Intention to Oppose:**
   Immediately file and serve a formal Notice of Intention to Oppose the Part B relief and the application to make the settlement agreements orders of court. This single procedural step will instantly remove the matter from the unopposed roll.

2. **Demand the Issued JH15:**
   Request the Registrar-stamped version of the JH15 Notice of Set Down from Elliott Attorneys to verify exactly what relief has been enrolled for 12 August.

### Short Term (Days 8–21)

3. **Finalize and File the Answering Affidavit:**
   The Part B filing pack (prepared on 11 June 2026) must be finalized, sworn, and filed. This affidavit must:
   - Address the merits of the s.162 delinquency claims.
   - Introduce the Mazars ISRS 4400 report (PF37/JH13) to prove it was an Agreed-Upon Procedures engagement, not a forensic audit proving misappropriation.
   - Expose the 99.6% sweep of entity funds by Peter.
   - Raise the defence of duress regarding the September 2025 settlement agreements.

4. **Launch the Counter-Application:**
   File the prepared counter-application concurrently with the answering affidavit. This should seek the setting aside of the original interdict (if the reconsideration track is abandoned) or formally align with the re-enrolled reconsideration application.

### Medium Term (Weeks 4–8)

5. **Consolidate the Litigation Tracks:**
   With the main application (Part B) now opposed, and the reconsideration application (Part A) pending re-enrolment, the respondents should seek a case management meeting to consolidate the hearings. It is highly inefficient and prejudicial to have the reconsideration of the interim order heard separately from the final relief.

6. **Address the 2026-115880 Collision:**
   Resolve the administrative collision where both Peter's stock-removal order (26 May) and Jax's reconsideration application (25 May) were issued under case number 2026-115880. Ensure all future filings in the main matter correctly cite 2025-137857.
