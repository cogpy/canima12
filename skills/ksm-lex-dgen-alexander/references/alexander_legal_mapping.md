# Alexander's 15 Properties → Legal Evidence Mapping

This reference document provides the complete mapping between Christopher Alexander's 15 fundamental properties of living structure and their instantiation within a legal-evidence context. Each property is defined in Alexander's original architectural domain, then translated to the legal domain with specific evaluation criteria and scoring heuristics.

## Evaluation Framework

Each property is scored on a scale of 0.0 to 1.0, where:

- **0.0–0.25**: Property absent — the legal structure lacks this quality entirely
- **0.25–0.50**: Property weak — present but underdeveloped
- **0.50–0.75**: Property moderate — functional but could be strengthened
- **0.75–1.00**: Property strong — fully realized and contributing to wholeness

The **Living Structure Score** is the geometric mean of all 15 property scores. A score above 0.70 indicates a legal case with genuine structural coherence.

---

## Property 1: LEVELS OF SCALE

**Alexander's Definition**: Centers exist at multiple scales, with a geometric ratio between scales (typically 2:1 to 3:1). Each level supports the next.

**Legal Mapping**: Evidence operates at micro (individual email, single transaction), meso (pattern of behavior, category of fraud), and macro (conspiracy, enterprise-level scheme) levels. A living legal structure has evidence at every scale, with each level reinforcing the others.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Does the case have evidence at the individual transaction level?
- Do individual events aggregate into discernible patterns?
- Do patterns compose into a coherent macro-level narrative?
- Is there a clear geometric progression from detail to overview?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = min(micro_evidence, meso_patterns, macro_narrative) × coherence_factor`

---

## Property 2: STRONG CENTERS

**Alexander's Definition**: The field has identifiable focal points — coherent entities that draw attention and organize the surrounding space.

**Legal Mapping**: Core allegations that are supported by overwhelming documentary proof. These are the "pillars" of the case — the arguments that, if proven, are individually sufficient to establish liability.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Are there 2-3 core allegations with documentary scores above 0.90?
- Does each strong center organize surrounding evidence around itself?
- Can the case survive the removal of any single center?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = (count_of_centers_above_0.85 / total_centers) × max_center_score`

---

## Property 3: BOUNDARIES

**Alexander's Definition**: Living centers are bounded by thick, visible boundaries that simultaneously separate and connect. The boundary itself is a center.

**Legal Mapping**: Clear delineation between entities, roles, and responsibilities. The corporate veil, directorial duties, and fiduciary obligations create boundaries that, when breached, constitute the legal wrong.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Are entity boundaries clearly established (CIPC records, MOIs)?
- Are role boundaries documented (employment contracts, board resolutions)?
- Is the breach of boundary clearly evidenced?
- Do the boundaries themselves tell a story (the veil piercing narrative)?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = boundary_clarity × breach_documentation × narrative_coherence`

---

## Property 4: ALTERNATING REPETITION

**Alexander's Definition**: Repeating centers alternate with contrasting elements, creating rhythm and reinforcement.

**Legal Mapping**: The pattern of repeated fraud across multiple channels, alternating with periods of concealment. Each instance of diversion (email to customer, bank account change, stock adjustment) repeats the same structural pattern but through a different channel.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Are there multiple independent instances of the same type of fraud?
- Do they alternate across different channels (banking, accounting, customer comms)?
- Does the repetition create a rhythm that makes the pattern undeniable?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = min(instance_count / 5, 1.0) × channel_diversity × temporal_spread`

---

## Property 5: POSITIVE SPACE

**Alexander's Definition**: Every part of space is positively shaped — there are no leftover, residual, or "dead" spaces.

**Legal Mapping**: Every element of the case serves a specific legal purpose. No evidence is included merely for volume; no argument is made without supporting facts. The "negative space" (what is NOT in the case) is also meaningful — the absence of board resolutions, the absence of a Power of Attorney.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Does every annexure serve a specific legal argument?
- Are there any "dead" sections that don't advance the case?
- Is negative evidence (absence of documents) explicitly leveraged?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = (purposeful_elements / total_elements) × negative_evidence_leverage`

---

## Property 6: GOOD SHAPE

**Alexander's Definition**: Individual centers have distinctive, recognizable shapes that are themselves composed of strong centers.

**Legal Mapping**: Each filing is well-formed and internally coherent. The affidavit follows proper legal structure (heading, parties, facts, law, relief). Each paragraph builds on the previous. The filing has a recognizable "shape" that a judge can follow.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Does each filing follow the prescribed legal format?
- Is the internal logic clear and progressive?
- Can the filing stand alone without external explanation?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = format_compliance × logical_progression × standalone_clarity`

---

## Property 7: LOCAL SYMMETRIES

**Alexander's Definition**: Small-scale symmetries exist throughout, even when the whole is asymmetric. Each center has its own internal balance.

**Legal Mapping**: Each sub-argument has its own internal balance — claim, evidence, law, conclusion. Even within an asymmetric case (where one party has far more evidence), each individual argument is balanced and complete.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Does each allegation have its own claim-evidence-law-conclusion structure?
- Are counter-arguments acknowledged and addressed?
- Is each sub-argument self-contained?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = avg(sub_argument_completeness) × counter_argument_coverage`

---

## Property 8: DEEP INTERLOCK AND AMBIGUITY

**Alexander's Definition**: Centers are woven together so that it is hard to tell where one ends and another begins. They share territory.

**Legal Mapping**: Evidence categories reinforce each other. The trust forgery evidence also supports the revenue diversion claim. The Sage sabotage evidence also proves premeditated entrapment. The same email proves multiple allegations simultaneously.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- How many evidence items support multiple allegations?
- Do the categories share "boundary evidence" that belongs to both?
- Does proving one allegation automatically strengthen another?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = cross_reference_density × mutual_reinforcement_factor`

---

## Property 9: CONTRAST

**Alexander's Definition**: Differentiation between adjacent centers is clear and sharp, creating vitality through opposition.

**Legal Mapping**: Clear distinction between lawful and unlawful conduct. The contrast between Daniel's documented business building and Peter/Rynette's documented sabotage. The contrast between what was told to the court and what actually happened.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Is the contrast between lawful and unlawful conduct stark?
- Are the "before" and "after" states clearly differentiated?
- Does the contrast create moral clarity?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = behavioral_contrast × temporal_contrast × moral_clarity`

---

## Property 10: GRADIENTS

**Alexander's Definition**: Qualities change gradually across space, creating smooth transitions and directional flow.

**Legal Mapping**: Escalation patterns from minor to major violations. The timeline shows a gradient from initial trust forgery (June 2024) through system sabotage (July 2024) to full revenue diversion (2024-2025) to court abuse (August 2025). Each step is more serious than the last.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Does the timeline show clear escalation?
- Is each step logically connected to the next?
- Does the gradient create a sense of inevitability?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = escalation_clarity × logical_connection × temporal_ordering`

---

## Property 11: ROUGHNESS

**Alexander's Definition**: Irregularities and imperfections that arise from adaptation to local conditions. Not sloppiness, but responsiveness.

**Legal Mapping**: Adaptation to specific facts rather than template-driven filings. The case responds to the unique circumstances — the family dynamics, the specific corporate structure, the particular South African legal context. It is not a generic fraud complaint.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Is the filing adapted to the specific facts?
- Does it acknowledge complexity and nuance?
- Are the arguments responsive to the actual evidence (not hypothetical)?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = fact_specificity × contextual_adaptation × nuance_acknowledgment`

---

## Property 12: ECHOES

**Alexander's Definition**: Deep similarities of form repeat throughout the structure, creating family resemblance and unity.

**Legal Mapping**: Recurring patterns across different fraud categories. The same modus operandi (impersonation, unauthorized access, diversion) appears in the trust forgery, the Sage capture, the banking changes, and the customer redirections. The "echo" is the consistent pattern of deception.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Does the same modus operandi appear across multiple categories?
- Are there "signature" behaviors that identify the perpetrators?
- Does the pattern create a sense of unified intent?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = pattern_recurrence × signature_consistency × intent_unity`

---

## Property 13: THE VOID

**Alexander's Definition**: At the heart of every living structure is an empty, still center — a place of calm that anchors everything around it.

**Legal Mapping**: The central truth that anchors the entire case. In the Revenue Stream Hijacking case, the void is: "Peter Faucitt and Rynette Farrar systematically hijacked the revenue streams of companies they did not own, using forged documents, system sabotage, and court abuse." Everything else orbits this central truth.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Can the entire case be stated in one sentence?
- Does that sentence remain true regardless of which evidence is challenged?
- Does the central truth create stillness (certainty) amid complexity?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = single_sentence_clarity × resilience_to_challenge × anchoring_power`

---

## Property 14: SIMPLICITY AND INNER CALM

**Alexander's Definition**: Despite complexity, the whole achieves a quality of quietness and geometric simplicity.

**Legal Mapping**: Despite the complexity of multiple entities, multiple fraud categories, and multiple legal proceedings, the core narrative remains clear and simple. A judge should be able to understand the case in 5 minutes, even if the detail takes 500 pages.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Can the case be explained to a layperson in 5 minutes?
- Does the executive summary capture the essence?
- Is the complexity organized rather than chaotic?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = executive_summary_clarity × organized_complexity × judicial_accessibility`

---

## Property 15: NOT-SEPARATENESS

**Alexander's Definition**: The structure is connected to its surroundings, growing from them, not imposed upon them.

**Legal Mapping**: The case is connected to broader legal frameworks and precedent. It grows from the Companies Act, the Trust Property Control Act, POPIA, and common law fraud — it is not an isolated complaint but part of a living legal ecosystem. The remedies sought are proportionate and connected to established legal principles.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Is the case grounded in established legal principles?
- Are the remedies proportionate and precedented?
- Does the case contribute to the development of the law?

**Scoring Heuristic**: `score = legal_grounding × proportionality × systemic_connection`

---

## Composite Living Structure Score

```python
import math

def living_structure_score(property_scores):
    """Geometric mean of all 15 property scores."""
    product = math.prod(property_scores.values())
    return product ** (1/15)
```

A score above **0.70** indicates a legal case with genuine structural coherence — one that a court will find compelling not just because of the evidence, but because of how the evidence is organized and presented.
